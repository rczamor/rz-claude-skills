# Frequency vs Quality — When Each Wins

## What it is
The core tension in creator content production: post frequently to feed the algorithm and maintain audience presence, OR invest deeply per piece to produce high-quality work that drives reshare and authority. Both matter; both have failure modes when over-indexed. The right answer is neither extreme — it's an explicit 80/20 split with each portion serving a different goal.

| Mode | Goal | Time per piece | Cadence | Output type |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Frequency posts (80% of cadence)** | Algorithm signal, audience presence, peer reshare seed | 20–45 min | 3–5x/week | Hot take, signal, story, framework |
| **Depth posts (20% of cadence)** | Authority, deep reshare, citation, evergreen | 4–8 hrs | 1x every 2 weeks | /thinking article, deep dive, framework essay |

For Riché's 5.25 hr/week budget, the math:
- 4 frequency posts × 30 min = 2 hrs
- 1 depth piece every 2 weeks averages = 2.5 hrs/week
- Engagement (commenting, DMs) = ~0.75 hrs/week
- Total ≈ 5.25 hrs

## Why it matters
The two extremes both fail:

**Frequency-only failure:** posting 5x/week with low investment per piece produces a feed that's present but forgettable. The algorithm sees activity (good); the audience sees nothing memorable (bad). Reshare rate stays low; peer recognition doesn't compound; the brand stays at audience-impression-level rather than reaching authority-citation-level. Common in 2025 — the LinkedIn AI-PM space is full of accounts posting daily generic insights with <2% reshare rate.

**Quality-only failure:** posting one deep piece every 6 weeks produces brilliant artifacts that the algorithm doesn't surface. Deep work without cadence loses the algorithm; the audience forgets you between pieces; the artifact may be excellent but it doesn't reach anyone. Common in 2024–2025 among academic-style PM thinkers who write monthly long-form essays that get 200 views.

The blended model works because each mode serves the other:
- Frequency posts feed the algorithm and signal "active creator," which makes the algorithm more likely to surface the eventual depth piece
- Depth pieces drive peer reshare and citation, which pulls Segment 1 (hiring managers) and Segment 4 (event organizers) attention back to the frequency posts
- Frequency posts can be derivatives of depth pieces (per `corpus/growth/strategy/repurposing-matrix.md`), reducing the average frequency-post cost

## How to apply

**Cadence allocation:**

Standard week (no major depth piece due):
- Mon: hot take (frequency, 30 min)
- Wed: signal post (frequency, 20 min)
- Fri: story (frequency, 30 min)

Depth-piece week (every 2nd week):
- Sun: ship the depth piece (the /thinking article, 4–6 hrs of work spread across the prior week)
- Mon: LinkedIn post derived from the depth piece (frequency, 20 min)
- Wed: LinkedIn carousel from the depth piece (frequency, 30 min)
- Fri: standalone hot take (frequency, 30 min)

The depth piece week reduces original frequency-post cost because half the frequency posts are derivatives.

**Quality bar by mode:**
- **Frequency posts:** must pass the voice check (`corpus/voice/`); must target a segment + comment angle; must have a clear takeaway or claim. Don't need to be original-thinking-grade.
- **Depth posts:** must contribute net-new thinking (not synthesis of existing thinking); must include a specific framework, model, or claim that's defensible; must be repurposable into 6+ derivatives.

**The "skip rather than ship low-quality" rule:**
When a frequency post is going to fall below the voice or substance bar, skip it rather than ship it. Cadence flexibility (3-vs-5 posts in a given week) is permitted; quality compromise is not. Per `corpus/growth/creator-dynamics/authentic-voice-as-moat.md` — voice integrity beats cadence consistency.

**The depth piece's role in the flywheel:**
The depth piece is what gets cited, reshared by Segment 2 to Segment 1, and what speakers/podcasters reference when introducing Riché. Without depth pieces, the brand is "active on LinkedIn" but not "the person who wrote the canonical piece on X." The 20% time investment is what differentiates being-known-from-being-recognized-as-authority.

## Examples
1. **Standard week.** 3 frequency posts (Mon hot take, Wed signal, Fri story) with the Wed post being a derivative of the prior month's depth piece on context engineering. Total cadence-related time: ~1.5 hrs.
2. **Depth-piece week.** Sun: ship the long /thinking article on agent memory architectures. Mon: LinkedIn post pulling the article's most counter-intuitive claim. Wed: LinkedIn carousel of the 4-stage framework from the article. Fri: standalone hot take. Article time: ~5 hrs (across the prior week's pre-work). Derivative time: ~75 min.
3. **Frequency-only failure (avoided).** A peer suggests posting 7x/week to "win the algorithm." Riché declines: the math breaks the budget AND voice quality drops. Stays at 3–5x/week with the depth-piece anchor.
4. **Quality-only failure (avoided).** A peer suggests "stop posting frequency content; just write 2-3 great essays per quarter." Riché declines: without frequency cadence, the algorithm forgets the account between essays, and essays land to ~10% of the audience they would otherwise.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating "more posts = more growth" as universally true. Past 5/week, returns drop sharply and voice quality erodes.
- Treating "fewer, better posts" as universally true. Without cadence, the algorithm doesn't surface even the best work.
- Skipping the depth piece for "easier" frequency content for >4 weeks. The brand stays present but stops compounding authority.
- Shipping a depth piece at 70% quality to hit a self-imposed cadence. Better to delay 1 week and ship at 95%.

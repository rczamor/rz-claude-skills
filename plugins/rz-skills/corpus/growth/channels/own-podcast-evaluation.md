# Own Podcast Channel Evaluation

## What it is
Hosting a podcast — distinct from guesting on others (which is owned by `corpus/growth/speaking/target-podcasts.md`). Hosting means: own the show, book the guests, edit the audio, distribute, market each episode. A 30-min episode costs roughly 4–6 hours end-to-end (booking + prep + recording + edit + show notes + distribution). Currently **not** the right channel — the production cost would consume the entire 5.25 hr/week budget for a single channel that's structurally redundant with guesting.

## Why it matters
Hosting flips the relationship: instead of being the guest who borrows audience, you become the host who *grants* audience access. This unlocks Segment 4 (event organizers, podcast hosts) and creates a credible reason to reach out to Segment 1 (VPs/CPOs). A well-positioned host podcast, even at small scale (sub-1K downloads/episode), is a recruiting tool for connections you couldn't otherwise make: "I'd love to have you on the show" outperforms "I'd love to chat" by ~5x in cold-outreach response rates.

The reason it's deferred isn't outcome — it's economics. At Riché's stage, *guesting* on existing podcasts produces equivalent reach for ~10% of the time investment. One Lenny's Podcast appearance reaches more of Segment 1 than 10 episodes of a brand-new host show would.

## When to launch (the gates)
All four must be true:

1. **≥10 podcast guesting appearances completed** (across Tier 1 and Tier 2 podcasts). Below this, the audience network needed to seed a host show isn't there.
2. **LinkedIn followers ≥5,000.** Hosts need a baseline platform to attract guests; below 5K, top guests decline.
3. **Newsletter launched and ≥1,000 subs.** The cross-promotion channel for episode releases. Without a newsletter, episode releases fizzle.
4. **A clear differentiated angle.** "PMs talking AI" is saturated. Riché's angle would need to be specific: e.g., "Practitioner architecture interviews — one production system, one founder, one hour." Generic "interviews with cool people" loses immediately.

If any gate is open, defer. Prefer guesting and reach the gates faster.

## How to apply (pre-launch)
- **Continue prioritizing guesting** per `corpus/growth/speaking/target-podcasts.md`. Each appearance teaches what works on the format and surfaces would-be guests for the eventual host show.
- **Track guests-as-potential-future-guests in HubSpot.** Every podcast guest you've shared a slot with (as a co-guest, in a panel, at a conference) is a warm ask when the host show launches.
- **Don't pre-announce a "podcast coming soon."** Same rule as newsletter — silence until launch.

## How to apply (post-launch — design rules)
- **Format: solo interview, 45–60 min, weekly.** Solo (not co-host) so cancellations don't kill episodes. Weekly so the cadence trains audience habit.
- **One angle, ruthlessly held.** Whatever the differentiated angle is from gate #4, every episode delivers it. Drift to "general PM advice" kills the show.
- **Production: ~4 hrs/episode.** Hard cap. Above this, the host burns out; below this, the quality drops.
- **Cross-promotion is the launch lever.** Every guest gets a launch package: pre-built LinkedIn post, X thread, newsletter snippet. Guest amplification > host amplification at every audience size.
- **Re-evaluate at episode 12 and episode 26.** Sustainable cadence test points.

## Examples
1. **Defer decision.** Q2 2026: 6 guesting appearances (4 below Tier 1), 1,180 LinkedIn followers, no newsletter. All four gates open. Continue guesting; revisit Q3.
2. **Launch quarter.** Q1 2027: 14 guesting appearances, 5,400 LinkedIn followers, newsletter at 1,800 subs, angle locked as "Production AI Architecture — one system, one builder." Launch with a 6-episode pre-recorded buffer to absorb the first cancellation.
3. **Cadence collapse.** After 8 weekly episodes, two scheduled guests cancel back-to-back. Riché records a solo "framework" episode (no guest) to hold the cadence. The show survives the gap.

## Anti-patterns
- Co-hosting with a friend "to split the work." Co-host scheduling is the #1 cause of podcast death. Solo or nothing.
- Launching without a 6-episode buffer. The first cancellation kills the cadence.
- "I'll just record short episodes to keep cadence." Short ≠ sustainable; the bottleneck is booking + editing, not recording length.
- Chasing famous guests in episode 1. The first 6 episodes set the show's identity; pick guests who'll bring substance, not name recognition.

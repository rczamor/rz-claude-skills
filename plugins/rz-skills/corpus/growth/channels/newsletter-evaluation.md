# Newsletter Channel Evaluation (Substack / Beehiiv / ConvertKit)

## What it is
A long-form email channel sent to subscribers on a fixed cadence. The strongest single channel for converting attention into a portable, owned asset (the email list). Currently deferred until LinkedIn followers ≥1,500 and inbound podcast/speaking invites ≥3 per month — see `corpus/growth/playbook/channel-gates-for-adding.md`. Until then, capture intent via the website signup form but do not launch.

| Platform | Best for | Cost | Portability |
|---|---|---|---|
| Substack | Discovery via Substack network, simple writer UX | $0 + 10% on paid subs | List exportable; Substack-network subscribers stay on Substack |
| Beehiiv | Built-in growth tools (referrals, recommendations), better analytics | $0 to ~3K subs, then ~$50/mo | Fully exportable; better for portability |
| ConvertKit / Kit | Mature creator tooling, sequences, tagging, monetization | $9-25/mo small, scales | Fully exportable; deepest creator features |

For Riché's stage (pre-launch, no current list, no monetization plan in 12 months): **Beehiiv** is the right pick if/when launched. Free tier covers the first ~3K subs; recommendations engine compounds growth via the broader Beehiiv network without the platform-lock risk Substack carries.

## Why it matters
Newsletter is the highest-leverage owned channel for a creator. Email is the only audience asset that survives platform shifts (LinkedIn algorithm changes, X policy changes, Google AI Overviews displacing search clicks). For Riché's positioning — practitioner-first thought leadership aimed at hiring managers and product leader peers — a monthly long-form deep dive matches how Segment 1 (VPs/CPOs) actually consume practitioner thinking: in their inbox on Sunday morning, not scrolling LinkedIn.

The deferral exists because launching a newsletter under 1,500 LinkedIn followers produces a list of 50–150 subs that's structurally too small to compound. The flywheel needs a baseline reach to seed enough opt-ins to reach the recommendation thresholds on Beehiiv (~500 subs unlocks meaningful recommendation traffic).

## When to launch (the gates)
All four must be true:

1. **LinkedIn followers ≥1,500.** Below this, the convertible top-of-funnel is too small.
2. **Inbound podcast/speaking invites ≥3/month for 2 consecutive months.** Indicates the brand has earned reach beyond the LinkedIn algo.
3. **At least 12 published /thinking articles on richezamor.com.** Source material for repurposing into newsletter issues without forcing fresh writing every cycle.
4. **A monthly cadence Riché can hold for 6 months.** Newsletters die on inconsistent cadence faster than on weak content.

If any gate is open, defer and revisit at quarterly review.

## How to apply (pre-launch)
- **Capture intent now.** The website signup form already exists; route signups into a holding list (no actual sends). When the newsletter launches, those subs become the seed.
- **Track signup velocity weekly.** A signup form averaging <2/week is a signal that the launch threshold is further out than the LinkedIn-follower count suggests.
- **Reserve `newsletter@richezamor.com`** as the from-address. Set up DKIM/SPF in advance so launch isn't blocked on deliverability setup.
- **Don't tease.** Don't announce the newsletter "is coming." Launch silently with the first issue when gates clear.

## How to apply (post-launch — design rules for when this skill activates)
- **Cadence: monthly, on the first Sunday of the month, 8 AM ET.** Higher cadence than monthly conflicts with the 5.25 hr/week budget; less than monthly loses subscriber engagement memory.
- **Format: long-form (1,500–2,500 words), one big idea per issue.** Each issue extends a /thinking article, not a tease of one.
- **Repurposing posture: newsletter → LinkedIn article → 3 LinkedIn posts.** The newsletter is the source, not a downstream artifact.
- **No referral mechanics in v1.** Add Beehiiv referrals only after 6 issues sustained.
- **Respect the unsubscribe.** Don't run win-back campaigns; they violate the practitioner-not-marketer voice.

## Examples
1. **Pre-launch quarterly review.** Q2 2026: LinkedIn followers at 1,180. Signup form averaging 1.4/week. Two podcast invites this quarter (below threshold). All four gates open; defer to Q3 review.
2. **Launch quarter.** Q4 2026: LinkedIn followers cross 1,650 in October. Three speaking invites in October alone. 14 /thinking articles published. Riché blocks November 1st (first Sunday) to ship Issue 1. Repurpose the most-reshared 2025 article as the seed.
3. **Cadence collapse recovery.** Issue 4 ships 3 weeks late after a heavy speaking month. Issue 5 ships on schedule with an explicit "back on cadence" note. No apology essay; just resume.

## Anti-patterns
- Launching with <500 baseline subs because "I have to start somewhere." Start when the funnel feeds it; the early loop matters more than the start date.
- Cross-posting LinkedIn posts as the newsletter body. Inbox is a different format; subscribers signed up for depth.
- Adding a paid tier in year one. Monetization is a separate decision; bolting it on before the free tier proves out poisons the unit economics.

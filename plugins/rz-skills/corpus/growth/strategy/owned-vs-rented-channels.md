# Owned vs Rented Channels — The 30% Owned-Channel Floor

## What it is
Every channel is one of two types:

- **Rented:** the platform owns the audience and the relationship (LinkedIn, X, YouTube, TikTok, Substack-network, Reddit). The platform can change the algorithm, the rules, the visibility, or the existence of your account at any time. Your audience is theirs.
- **Owned:** you own the audience and the relationship (richezamor.com, an email list, a downloadable subscriber file, a podcast RSS feed you control). The platform you use can change, but the audience moves with you.

**The rule: a creator brand should allocate at least 30% of growth time to owned channels at all times, regardless of how well rented channels are performing.** The 30% is not a recommendation; it's insurance.

## Why it matters
For a creator brand, platform risk is product risk. Rented channels — LinkedIn, X, YouTube — can wipe out years of work in days through algorithm changes, policy shifts, or platform decisions you have no input on. Examples observed in 2024–2025:

- LinkedIn's algorithm change in mid-2024 cut organic reach for many practitioner accounts by 40–70%.
- X's policy shifts after the 2022 acquisition broke many established creator workflows.
- YouTube's monetization rule changes in 2025 demonetized entire content categories overnight.
- Substack's recommendation network changes shifted who got discovered and who didn't.

Each of these moved entire creator businesses from "thriving" to "rebuilding" within a quarter. The creators who survived had owned channels — email lists, websites, direct community — that gave them a base to rebuild from. The creators who didn't, didn't.

For Riché's positioning specifically — practitioner-first, multi-segment, multi-quarter career-defining brand — the rented channels (LinkedIn, X) are the primary surfaces today, but they cannot be the *only* surfaces. The 30% floor on owned channels is a structural defense, not a tactical preference.

## How to apply

**Audit current allocation:**

Rented (current ~85%):
- LinkedIn (~2.5 hrs/week)
- X cross-posting (~1.25 hrs)
- LinkedIn strategic commenting (~1.75 hrs)
- Reddit / Discord / X engagement (within commenting block)

Owned (current ~15%, BELOW FLOOR):
- richezamor.com writing (rolls into Sunday batch, ~30 min equivalent)
- richezamor.com signup form (passive)

**This violates the 30% floor.** The current allocation has Riché over-indexed on rented channels with insufficient owned-channel investment. The corrective actions, in priority order:

1. **Increase richezamor.com cadence to ≥1 article/month with explicit time block.** Move the article work out of the Sunday batch overflow and into a named ~45 min/week block.
2. **Activate the email-capture loop.** Even pre-newsletter, the signup form should be a primary CTA on every /thinking article and the about page.
3. **Launch the newsletter when gates clear** (per `corpus/growth/channels/newsletter-evaluation.md`) — this is the single biggest lever for moving toward 30%.
4. **Treat GitHub repos as owned-adjacent.** GitHub is technically rented (Microsoft owns it) but the artifacts are portable, downloadable, and forkable; behaves more like owned than LinkedIn does.

**Decision rule for new channels:** when scoring a candidate channel via `corpus/growth/strategy/channel-evaluation-framework.md`, apply a 1.2x multiplier to the score if the channel is owned. This biases the evaluation toward owned-channel investment and against further rented-channel sprawl.

**Quarterly check at review:** calculate the actual owned/rented allocation. If owned <30%, the next quarter's planning prioritizes owned-channel investment over any new rented-channel addition.

## Owned-channel hierarchy (most → least portable)

1. **Email list (DKIM-authenticated, fully exportable).** The most portable asset; survives any platform change.
2. **Self-hosted website with direct-traffic acquisition.** Compounds via SEO; portable across hosts.
3. **Owned podcast RSS feed.** Distributed by Spotify/Apple but the feed is yours.
4. **GitHub repos with permissive licenses.** Forkable; survive GitHub-the-platform.
5. **Self-hosted community (Discord with backed-up data, Discourse forum).** Portable with effort.

Anything not on this list is rented, regardless of how it feels.

## Examples
1. **30% floor restoration plan.** Current: 15% owned. Target by Q1 2027: 30%. Path: monthly newsletter (60 min/week), weekly /thinking article (45 min/week), GitHub artifact per article (30 min/week). Funded by: dropping daily X engagement to 3x/week (saves 30 min/week) and consolidating LinkedIn commenting block (saves 15 min/week).
2. **Platform-risk realized.** Hypothetical: LinkedIn algo change in mid-2026 cuts Riché's reach by 50%. With owned-channel floor at 30%, the email list (~1,800 subs) and the website (~5K monthly visits) absorb the disruption. Recovery plan exists. Without the floor, the brand would be in crisis.
3. **Failed allocation defense.** A peer suggests doubling LinkedIn cadence to recover from a slow quarter. The owned-channel allocation is already at 15% (below floor). Riché declines the LinkedIn-first response and instead doubles down on the newsletter launch path.

## Anti-patterns
- "I'll worry about owned channels once rented is bigger." The bigger the rented base, the bigger the platform risk. Build owned in parallel.
- Counting LinkedIn newsletter as an owned channel. The subscriber list is not exportable in a useful form; LinkedIn owns the relationship.
- Treating downloadable PDFs as a substitute for an email list. PDFs without a captured email don't reach the reader again.

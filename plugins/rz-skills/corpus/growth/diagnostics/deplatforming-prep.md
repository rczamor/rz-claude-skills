# Deplatforming Preparation — The Plan B

## What it is
The contingency plan for when a primary rented channel becomes unusable: account suspended, terminated, severely throttled, or made unviable through policy changes. Rare but high-cost — losing LinkedIn or X without a Plan B can set the brand back 12–24 months. The Plan B is not crisis response; it's pre-positioned infrastructure that activates within 48 hours of disruption.

## Why it matters
Deplatforming risk is structural in 2025–2026. Every major platform has terminated, throttled, or restricted accounts that the operators believed were safe. Causes observed:

- **False-positive policy enforcement.** AI-powered moderation flagging legitimate content as violating policy.
- **Mass-flagging campaigns.** Coordinated reporting by detractors triggering automated review.
- **Policy changes affecting entire categories.** "AI-generated content" labels, "creator content" restrictions.
- **Acquisition or pivot.** Platform changes ownership or business model in ways that break creator workflows.
- **Legitimate violations Riché doesn't realize he's making.** Misunderstood guidelines, evolving rules.

For Riché's brand, the highest-stakes deplatforming would be LinkedIn (primary channel; Segment 1 + Segment 2 reach). X is secondary; richezamor.com is owned (can't be deplatformed by an external party); GitHub is platform-rented but artifacts are portable.

The Plan B doesn't prevent deplatforming. It bounds the recovery cost from "12–24 months of lost work" to "8–12 weeks of inconvenience."

## How to apply

**Pre-positioned infrastructure (built once, maintained quarterly):**

1. **Email list with full export capability.** The most important Plan B asset. See `corpus/growth/strategy/audience-portability.md`. If LinkedIn vanishes, the email list is the only direct line to the audience.
2. **richezamor.com as the canonical home.** All content links back; the bio on every other platform points to richezamor.com; the website does not depend on any platform.
3. **Backup of LinkedIn content.** Quarterly export of every post, every connection (CSV), every conversation thread. Stored in a cloud backup separate from LinkedIn.
4. **Backup of X content.** Same: quarterly export of tweets, threads, DM history, follower list.
5. **Direct contact info for top 50 connections.** Email, occasionally phone, of the 50 most-important connections (top peers in Segment 2, key Segment 4 contacts). Maintained in HubSpot. If LinkedIn vanishes, these 50 are reachable directly within 24 hours.
6. **A "next platform" plan documented.** Not pre-built (overkill), but documented: if LinkedIn becomes unviable, the next-priority channel is X + newsletter + speaking. If X becomes unviable, the next-priority is LinkedIn + newsletter + LinkedIn newsletter. Decision tree pre-made.

**The 48-hour activation playbook (when deplatforming hits):**

Hour 0–4:
- Confirm the disruption. Test from a logged-out browser; check status pages; check creator forums.
- If account restoration looks possible: file appeals through official channels. Don't post about it publicly.
- If restoration unlikely: proceed to next steps.

Hour 4–24:
- Send an email to the holding list / newsletter list (if active) explaining the situation in 2 sentences. Direct to richezamor.com.
- Post a brief richezamor.com blog post: "Where to find me now" with links to remaining channels.
- DM the top 50 connections directly (email if no DM channel) with the same info.

Hour 24–48:
- Activate the next-priority channel from the documented plan. Post the standard cadence on the new primary channel.
- Reach out to 5-10 supportive peers asking them to share the "where to find me now" post.

Week 2-4:
- Rebuild cadence on remaining channels.
- Increase newsletter cadence temporarily (if newsletter active) to maintain audience contact.
- Re-evaluate channel mix in the next quarterly review.

**Quarterly maintenance (15 min/quarter):**
- Export LinkedIn data (posts, connections)
- Export X data
- Verify richezamor.com email signups still routing correctly
- Confirm top-50 contact list in HubSpot is current
- Verify newsletter platform export still works

## Severity levels and response intensity

| Severity | Example | Response |
|---|---|---|
| **Throttled** | Algo change reduces reach by 30-50% | Use `algorithm-change-response.md`; deplatforming Plan B not activated |
| **Restricted** | Specific content types blocked or warned | Adapt formats per `algorithm-change-response.md`; Plan B prepared but not activated |
| **Suspended (recoverable)** | Account suspended pending review (days–weeks) | File appeal; pause publishing; no public commentary; Plan B prepared, partially activated |
| **Terminated (unrecoverable)** | Permanent account closure with no recourse | Full Plan B activation per playbook above |

## Examples
1. **Quarterly drill (well-handled).** Q1 2026: Riché runs the Plan B drill. Exports LinkedIn (847 posts, 1,180 connections), exports X (1,400 tweets, 320 followers), verifies email routing on richezamor.com, updates HubSpot top-50 list. 22 minutes total. No actual disruption; the drill is the insurance.
2. **Hypothetical false-positive suspension.** A LinkedIn moderation false-positive in Q2 2026 suspends Riché's account for 6 days during appeal. Action: standard appeal filed; no public commentary; X cadence increases to 5x/week temporarily; newsletter sends a "see you on X" mention. Account reinstated; recovery: 1 week.
3. **Hypothetical worst-case.** A 2027 platform decision permanently restricts AI-PM content. Riché activates full Plan B: emails the list, posts the "where to find me" page, DMs top 50, pivots to newsletter + speaking + X as primary mix. Estimated recovery to prior-impact baseline: 9 months.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating deplatforming as too unlikely to plan for. The cost of preparation is small; the cost of being unprepared is enormous.
- Public commentary during a suspension/restriction. Reduces appeal odds; signals operator frustration.
- Letting backups go stale. A 2-year-old export of LinkedIn data is worth roughly nothing if deplatforming hits today.
- Skipping the quarterly drill because "everything is fine." The drill IS the preparation; without it, the plan is only theoretical.

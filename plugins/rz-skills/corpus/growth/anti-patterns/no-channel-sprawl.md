# Don't Sprawl Channels — Every Add Requires a Cut

## What it is
The rule: never add a new channel to the active set without explicitly retiring or downgrading an existing one. The 5.25 hr/week budget is fixed; adding a channel without cutting one creates time debt that compounds into either burnout or quality collapse on every channel.

## Why it matters
Channel sprawl is the most common failure mode for personal brands at the 1K–10K follower stage. The pattern: LinkedIn is working, so the creator adds X. X gets some traction, so they add a newsletter. The newsletter looks promising, so they add YouTube. Within 6 months, every channel runs at 50% of optimal cadence, none compound, the time budget breaks, and the creator burns out blaming "audience fatigue" instead of the real cause: undisciplined channel addition.

For Riché specifically, the math is binding. The current 5.25 hours runs at 90%+ utilization across LinkedIn (writing + engagement), X (cross-post), website articles, speaking prep, and admin. Adding any channel without removing something means one of the existing channels degrades. A degraded LinkedIn cadence (the primary channel where Segment 2 lives) costs more than any plausible new channel gains.

The discipline is not "no new channels ever." It's "no new channels without an explicit, named retirement."

## How to apply
**Before adding any channel — three explicit answers required:**

1. **What channel or activity am I removing or downgrading?** Specific. Not "I'll find time"; not "I'll be more efficient." A named cut: "I'm dropping X cross-posting" or "I'm dropping the Friday admin block to 5 min."
2. **What's the time delta?** New channel projected hrs/week minus retired activity hrs/week. If positive, the math doesn't work — pick a bigger cut or a smaller new channel.
3. **What's the test condition for reversing?** "If LinkedIn engagement drops >25% in 6 weeks, I revert." Pre-committed reversal criteria prevent slow drift into channel sprawl.

**The "easier than removing" trap:** Adding feels easier than cutting because cuts feel like loss. But the loss is invisible (the *not-degraded* primary channel) while the gain is visible (the new channel's first wins). Override this with the discipline: every add requires a documented cut.

**Quarterly review enforces the rule.** At each quarterly channel review (`corpus/growth/playbook/quarterly-channel-review.md`), check: did any channel get added since last quarter? If yes, what was cut? If nothing was cut, the channel set is in sprawl; force a retirement.

## What counts as "adding a channel"
- New publishing surface (newsletter, YouTube, podcast hosting, TikTok)
- New regular engagement surface (Reddit, Discord, Mastodon)
- New activity (cold outreach campaigns, speaker pitching, paid promotion)

What does NOT count (no cut required):
- Cross-posting an existing piece (LinkedIn → X derivative)
- One-shot launches (Show HN, a single guest post)
- Increasing cadence on an existing channel (still requires budget check, but not a channel cut)

## Examples
1. **Newsletter consideration with cut.** Riché evaluates newsletter launch in Q3 2026. Time projection: 1 hr/week. The cut: drop the Tuesday signal post on LinkedIn (frees ~30 min) AND reduce Sunday writing block from 2 hrs to 90 min by tightening the writing process. Net delta: 0 hrs. Reversal condition: LinkedIn weekly engagement holds within 15%.
2. **Channel sprawl avoided.** A peer suggests adding Bluesky. Riché evaluates: ~1.5 hrs/week to do well. The cut required is significant (drop X cross-post). Bluesky audience overlap with Segment 2 is unproven. Decision: defer; revisit when Bluesky has measurable Segment 2 density.
3. **Failed sprawl (lesson).** Q4 2025: Riché added Discord engagement informally (~45 min/week) without cutting anything. Within 8 weeks, the Sunday writing block ran 30 min over consistently and 2 weeks of LinkedIn missed the Wednesday post. Lesson logged: undocumented adds always produce drift.

## Anti-patterns
- "I'll just try the new channel for a month." Trial periods become permanent without a hard reversal date.
- Counting "I'll be more efficient" as a cut. Efficiency gains are real but not bankable in advance.
- Treating informal engagement as not-a-channel. 30 min/week on Discord IS a channel; budget it accordingly.

# Don't Invest in TikTok or Instagram Reels

## What it is
The rule: don't produce content for TikTok, Instagram Reels, or YouTube Shorts. Short-form vertical video as a primary channel — distinct from a 90-second cross-post clip from a longer talk, which is a *derivative* not a *channel*.

## Why it matters
The audience density is wrong. None of the 4 segments live on TikTok or Reels:

- **Segment 1 (AI Product Hiring Managers, VP/CPO-level).** Vanishingly small TikTok presence. They consume on LinkedIn and via inbox.
- **Segment 2 (Product Leader Peers, PRIMARY).** A small minority use TikTok, but not for professional consumption. The peers Riché needs to reach are on LinkedIn.
- **Segment 3 (AI Founders, Seed–Series B).** Builders consume on X, HN, and GitHub. TikTok is irrelevant.
- **Segment 4 (Event Organizers + Podcast Hosts).** Discover speakers through LinkedIn and podcast guesting, not short-form video.

The format is also wrong for the positioning. Context-architecture content is structurally long-form: it requires a setup, a tension, a specific demonstration, and a lesson. Compressing this to 60 seconds either oversimplifies (damaging the practitioner-not-theorist hook) or trends-bait (damaging the brand entirely). The cost is high (production for short-form vertical is 4–8 hours per finished minute) and the reach payoff misses the audience.

There's a separate, smaller-stakes case for short-form *clips* derived from talks or YouTube videos. Those are repurposing, not original-channel investment, and they go on LinkedIn (native video) and X — not TikTok.

## How to apply
- **Refuse short-form vertical as a content channel.** No TikTok account, no Reels account, no YouTube Shorts schedule.
- **Permit derivative clips on LinkedIn and X.** A 60–90 second clip from a podcast appearance, talk, or article-recording can be cross-posted natively to LinkedIn and X. These are derivatives of work already produced; they consume <30 min each.
- **If a peer or recruiter says "you should be on TikTok," decline politely.** "The audience I'm building toward isn't on TikTok; the time goes further on LinkedIn and richezamor.com."
- **Re-evaluate quarterly only if Segment 2 measurably migrates.** Not on individual cases; on systematic audience movement. Currently zero signal of migration.

## Examples
1. **A creator-coaching peer suggests TikTok for "audience growth."** Riché declines: "My audience is on LinkedIn; spending the time there compounds 5x faster."
2. **A 12-minute talk recording gets a 75-second clip cross-posted to LinkedIn and X.** Permitted; this is derivative content from a primary channel asset, not TikTok investment.
3. **An invitation to be a "creator" in an AI-PM TikTok series.** Decline. The series may have audience, but it's not Riché's audience.

## Anti-patterns
- "But Lenny is on TikTok now." Lenny has 6-figure audiences across all channels and a team; the channel calculus is different at scale. Riché's stage is not Lenny's stage.
- Cross-posting LinkedIn videos to TikTok "just in case." Each platform has its own algorithmic expectations; copy-paste fails on both.
- Treating Shorts as separate from Reels/TikTok. They share format, audience, and time cost. The rule applies uniformly.

# Don't Copy-Paste the Same Post Across Platforms

## What it is
The rule: never publish the identical post on LinkedIn and X (or LinkedIn and any other platform). Each platform has its own format expectations, audience norms, and algorithmic preferences. Cross-posting requires *adaptation*, not copy-paste.

## Why it matters
Both algorithms penalize cross-posted content. LinkedIn's algorithm de-prioritizes posts that match URL signatures or text fingerprints from external platforms; X's algorithm down-ranks tweets that look like they were composed for elsewhere. Beyond the algo cost, the audience cost is bigger:

- LinkedIn rewards line-broken, scannable, conversational structure with a clear takeaway. ~150–250 words.
- X rewards punchy first-line, threaded breakdown, demo-first hooks. <280 chars per tweet, threads 5–9 tweets.
- The same content forced into both formats lands well in neither.

The audience overlap between LinkedIn and X is also smaller than it looks. The followers who see a copy-pasted post on both feeds notice the laziness, which signals "marketer" rather than "practitioner." For a brand that depends on the practitioner positioning, this signal is expensive.

## How to apply
**The adaptation discipline (per `corpus/growth/playbook/x-cross-post-engagement.md`):**

For every LinkedIn post that gets adapted to X:
1. **Reframe the hook.** LinkedIn allows a 2-sentence setup; X demands the punchline in line 1.
2. **Restructure for the format.** A LinkedIn 250-word post becomes a 6-tweet thread on X. Same idea, different shape.
3. **Adjust the close.** LinkedIn closes with a question or CTA; X closes with a re-tweetable summary or a link.
4. **Re-engage with the X-native audience.** Reply to comments on X with X-conventions (terse, threaded), not LinkedIn-conventions (warm, expansive).

The 15-minute daily X block (`corpus/growth/playbook/x-cross-post-engagement.md`) covers this adaptation work. It's not free; budget for it.

**What's allowed (true derivatives):**
- A 90-sec clip from a YouTube video posted natively to LinkedIn AND X — derivative artifact, not a copy-pasted post.
- A LinkedIn carousel deck posted as a separate X thread with the same images — different format, same source content.
- A /thinking article linked on both with platform-adapted intros — link is identical, intro copy differs.

## Examples
1. **Adaptation done right.** Monday LinkedIn post: "I've been wrong about RAG for 18 months. The thing I missed was…" 240 words, framework-led, ends with a question. Tuesday X thread: tweet 1 "I was wrong about RAG for 18 months 🧵" tweet 2-6 the framework, tweet 7 link to the LinkedIn post. Same idea, two correctly-shaped artifacts.
2. **Copy-paste failure.** Early experiment: identical 220-word LinkedIn post pasted into a single tweet (truncated at char 280, ellipses cutting off the close). On LinkedIn it performed at baseline; on X it performed at ~10% of baseline. Lesson logged.
3. **Edge case — short, native-style post.** A 40-word punchy take on an industry move. Posted to both platforms with minor first-line tweaks (X version drops the LinkedIn salutation). Acceptable because it's short enough that adaptation costs more than the algo penalty saves.

## Anti-patterns
- Using a tool that auto-cross-posts. Auto-cross-post tools are categorically banned per `corpus/growth/anti-patterns/no-buffer-typefully.md`.
- Adapting only the formatting (line breaks) but keeping identical text. The fingerprint is in the text content; the algos see through superficial reformatting.
- Cross-posting then deleting one when it underperforms. Algos don't forget the duplicate; deletions don't recover the penalty.

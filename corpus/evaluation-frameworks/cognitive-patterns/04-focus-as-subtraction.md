# Focus as Subtraction (Jobs)

## What it is
The primary value-add of a CEO is what they say no to. When Jobs returned to Apple, he reduced the product line from 350 SKUs to 10. Not because the other 340 were bad ideas — because they were diluting the 10 that mattered. Focus is not a posture or a slogan. It is a deletion operation performed continuously on the roadmap, the org chart, the meeting calendar, and every plan that crosses the desk.

## Why it matters
Plans expand by default. Every reviewer adds a concern, every stakeholder adds a feature, every retrospective adds a process. Without a CEO who instinctively subtracts, plans grow until the team can no longer do any of them well. The 10x CEO treats every "and we should also..." as a candidate for deletion, not addition. Default to fewer things, done better.

## How to apply
1. After reading a plan, ask: "If I could only ship 30% of this, what 30% would I keep?" Then ask whether the other 70% should ship at all.
2. For every feature in the plan, ask: "What happens if we cut this and ship the rest?" If the answer is "fine," cut it.
3. Resist the gravitational pull of "while we're in there, we might as well..." That's how a 3-day plan becomes a 3-month plan.
4. Defer aggressively. Most "we need this" is "we want this." Most "we want this" is "we'd be embarrassed not to have this." None of those are reasons to ship.

## Examples
1. **Product Hunt launch plan** with hero video, blog post, email sequence, partner co-marketing, paid ads, and conference timing. Subtraction: ship the launch with the video and one tweet. Defer the rest. Did the launch succeed? Then maybe nothing else was needed. Did it underperform? Then you know which lever to pull next.
2. **Internal admin tool** with 14 dashboards. Ask: which 2 do operators actually open? Build those. Delete the other 12 from scope.
3. **Onboarding flow** with 9 steps including "tour the dashboard." Cut to 4 steps. Tour is not onboarding — it is delay.

## Anti-patterns
- Adding "phase 2" placeholders for everything you cut. The cut is real or it isn't. "Phase 2 someday" is just a slow ship.
- Confusing focus with austerity. Focus is doing fewer things at maximum quality. Austerity is doing fewer things badly.

# Above-the-Fold Communicates Purpose in 3 Seconds

## What it is

The content above the fold (visible without scrolling) must answer three questions in three seconds:

1. **What is this?** (Product / page identity)
2. **Why should I care?** (Value proposition)
3. **What should I do?** (Primary CTA)

If a user can't answer all three from a glance — without scrolling, hovering, or thinking — the above-the-fold has failed.

## Why it matters

Users decide whether to stay or leave within seconds. Most never scroll past the first viewport on a marketing page. If the fold doesn't communicate what the product is and why they should care, they leave before discovering the rest of the site. The fold is the entire pitch for a huge fraction of visitors.

This applies to product pages too — the top of a settings page, dashboard, or doc page should make clear what it is and what the user can do here.

## How to apply

1. **Headline: what + why in one sentence.** Not "Welcome to X." More like "Process payments online in any currency." (Stripe).
2. **Subhead or short body: clarify the why.** One or two lines.
3. **One primary CTA visible.** "Start free trial" / "View pricing" / "Get started."
4. **Test it.** Show the fold to someone unfamiliar. After 3 seconds, ask: "What is this and what would you do?"

## Examples

- **GOOD:** Stripe's homepage — "Financial infrastructure to grow your revenue" + brief subhead + "Start now" button. 3 seconds to comprehension.
- **BAD:** A landing page with a hero image of a smiling team and "Welcome to Acme — we're building the future of work." User has no idea what the product does.
- **GOOD:** Linear — "Linear is a purpose-built tool for planning and building products" + product screenshot + "Get started." Identity, value, action — all visible.
- **BAD:** A SaaS hero with a vague headline ("Empower your team") and three CTAs ("Learn more", "Watch demo", "Sign up"). Identity unclear, action unclear.

## Anti-patterns

- Burying the value prop in a paragraph below an "About us" section. The fold is for the user, not the founder.
- Hero image so dominant that the headline is barely visible. The image is decoration; the headline is information.

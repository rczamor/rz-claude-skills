# Neural Architect Overview

## What it is
The "Neural Architect" is Riché's unifying visual identity. Every asset — slide, social graphic, diagram, website panel — should look like it came from the same system: technical, precise, dark-themed, and architecturally structured. The persona is a practitioner who builds the systems, not a vendor who markets them. The visual cues that signal this: dark backgrounds, monospace alongside clean sans, node-and-edge structures, and accents reserved for the load-bearing element rather than ornament.

## Why it matters
Personal brands win on consistency. When a viewer sees a Riché diagram in a LinkedIn feed, they should recognize it before they read the caption. The Neural Architect identity is also a filter against generic AI imagery and Canva-default polish — the things that signal "talker about AI" rather than "builder of AI." Every visual choice is calibrated to make Riché look like the person who shipped the system, not the person who blogged about it.

## How to apply
- **Default to dark.** Backgrounds in the #0a0a0a–#1a1a1a range. Cards and panels lift to #2a2a2a.
- **One accent per project.** Pick electric blue, emerald green, or amber. Use it for the load-bearing node, the highlighted comparison, the data point — never decoration.
- **Architectural over organic.** Geometric shapes, clean stroke widths, node-and-edge diagrams. Never hand-drawn, gradient mesh, or rounded blobs.
- **Code and diagrams as anchors.** When a slide or post needs visual weight, reach for a code snippet or an architecture diagram before reaching for a stock illustration.
- **IDE-feel for marks.** Any logo or favicon should look like a developer-tool icon, not a SaaS lifestyle brand.

## Examples
1. **Five-step context flow diagram on LinkedIn.** Dark background, monospace stage labels, electric-blue highlight on the synthesis node, "richezamor" attribution in the lower-right corner at 60% opacity.
2. **Conference talk title slide.** Near-black background, white headline in Inter, monospace subtitle in JetBrains Mono giving the framework name, no decorative imagery.
3. **richezamor.com hero panel.** Dark gray card on near-black background, single accent color used in the live demo, no gradients, no stock photography.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating "Neural Architect" as a tagline rather than a design system — the words appear nowhere, the look is what carries it.
- Mixing the dark technical palette with bright marketing illustrations because a single asset "needed energy." If energy is needed, it comes from the diagram structure, not the color temperature.

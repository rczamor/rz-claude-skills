# Imagery Anti-Patterns

## What it is
Three categories of imagery that are categorically banned from every Riché asset, because each one signals a position that contradicts the Neural Architect identity:

1. **Stock photos of people pointing at screens / handshakes / lifestyle business shots.**
2. **Generic AI imagery — robots, glowing brains, neural-network mesh wallpapers, circuit-board backgrounds, "AI" rendered in chrome.**
3. **Clip art and Canva-default visual elements — flat illustrations of office workers, generic icons used as decoration, template motion graphics.**

## Why it matters
Each banned category broadcasts the wrong audience. Stock photos broadcast "consultant brand." Generic AI imagery broadcasts "talker about AI, not builder of AI." Clip art and Canva defaults broadcast "I do not have a visual identity, so I picked the platform's suggestions." The audience Riché is building toward — operators, technical PMs, founders, conference programmers — filters those signals out faster than they read the caption. Riché's visual identity is the gatekeeper that gets him into the tab they actually read.

## How to apply
- **No people-in-an-office imagery.** No handshakes, no over-the-shoulder laptop shots, no "team smiling around a whiteboard," no "professional pointing at a chart." If a person needs to appear, it's Riché's headshot in a clearly attributed context — not generic stock.
- **No "AI" visual cliches.** No glowing brain. No humanoid robot. No neural-network mesh as wallpaper. No circuit-board texture. No chrome "AI" lettering. No abstract data swirl.
- **No clip art / Canva defaults.** No flat-illustration office workers, no generic "lightbulb = idea" icons, no template-shaped motion overlays. If an icon is needed, it comes from a single curated library (Lucide, Phosphor, Heroicons).
- **No decorative emoji as design.** Emoji can appear in copy but never as a design element substituting for an icon or label.

## Examples
1. **Rejected: glowing-brain header on a "How AI Changes PM" post.** Replace with a node-and-edge diagram of the actual workflow change. The diagram is the argument; the brain is decoration.
2. **Rejected: stock photo of two people shaking hands on an "AI partnerships" slide.** Replace with a layer diagram showing where each partner fits in the architecture.
3. **Rejected: Canva-template "tech-themed" social card with hexagon mesh background.** Replace with a flat dark canvas, the framework name in monospace, and the actual diagram doing the work.

## Anti-patterns
- "But the article needs a hero image, and we don't have a diagram yet." Then ship the article with a clean typographic title card, not a stock photo placeholder.
- Reaching for glowing-brain imagery "ironically" or "as a critique of AI hype." The visual cliche reads at thumbnail before the irony lands.

# Diagrams Over Abstract Graphics

## What it is
For social graphics, diagrams and frameworks beat abstract decorative imagery — every time. Riché's audience wants substance in visuals, not aesthetic decoration. A Five-Step Context Flow diagram outperforms a glowing-AI-brain illustration because the diagram is the post; the brain is wrapper.

## Why it matters
Abstract graphics ask the audience to do interpretive work without paying off. The viewer sees a swirl of color and a bold headline; they get a vibe but no idea, and they scroll on. A diagram, by contrast, is itself an idea. The viewer can pause, parse the structure, and walk away with the framework in mind even if they didn't read the caption. That's the asymmetry that makes diagrams compound: each post leaves a tool in the audience's head, not just an impression.

## How to apply
- **Default first move: "what's the diagram for this post?"** Before designing anything, ask whether the post's argument can be drawn as a system, a framework, or a comparison. If yes, that's the visual.
- **No abstract textures or gradient backgrounds.** Flat dark canvas, structural visual on top.
- **No "concept art" illustrations.** No glowing brains, no swirling data streams, no humanoid robots, no neural-network mesh wallpapers.
- **Use the canonical diagram set.** Five-Step Context Flow, RAG vs. Context Layer, Context Quality Framework, Flywheel — these get reused. New post arguments either fit one of these or generate a new diagram in the same vocabulary.
- **When the post is a quote, render the quote as the diagram.** Type set carefully on a clean canvas, a small visual anchor (a geometric mark, a single icon) — that's the visual. The quote is the substance; a "concept illustration" of the quote dilutes it.

## Examples
1. **Strong: a post about context drift, with the Context Quality Framework radar chart showing a "before" and "after" state.** The visual is the argument. The audience sees the dimension that improved.
2. **Strong: a quote post — "Retrieval is the first step, not the last." Set in Inter Bold 80pt on `#0a0a0a`, with a small geometric mark in the corner.** The typography and composition carry it. No need for an "abstract retrieval visualization."
3. **Weak: a post about AI with a glowing-brain illustration as the visual.** The illustration is decorative; cut it and use a diagram or strong typographic treatment instead.

## Anti-patterns
- "But the post needs a hero image, and there's no diagram yet." Then ship a typographic hero — clean type on the dark canvas, single accent. That's a diagram in disguise.
- Adding an abstract graphic "for visual interest" alongside a diagram. The diagram is the visual interest; the abstract addition steals attention.

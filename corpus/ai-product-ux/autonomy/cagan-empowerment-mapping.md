# Cagan Empowerment Mapping

## What it is
A concept that maps the AI-product progressive-autonomy pattern directly onto Marty Cagan's empowerment framework from product strategy. **Progressive autonomy in AI products = the trust-building pattern in product strategy.** The mechanics are the same; only the actor changes. In product strategy, leadership grants autonomy to teams as they demonstrate outcomes. In AI products, users grant autonomy to the AI as it demonstrates accuracy. Same loop, different scale.

## Why it matters
Riché works across product management *and* product design. Recognizing the same pattern in both domains is what lets a single principle organize a roadmap. When a PM understands that the trust-building dynamic between leadership and team is structurally identical to the dynamic between user and AI, both designs improve. The PM stops shipping "AI features" that demand binary trust; the designer stops shipping autonomy interfaces that ignore organizational analogues.

## How to apply
1. **When designing AI autonomy, consult `corpus/pm-frameworks/strategy/empowered-vs-feature-teams.md`.** The shape of the team-empowerment ladder predicts the shape of the AI-empowerment ladder.
2. **Apply the trust-barrier framework symmetrically.** See `corpus/pm-frameworks/strategy/trust-barrier.md`. The barriers leadership puts up to delegating outcomes are the same barriers users put up to delegating AI actions.
3. **Use the cross-link to teach.** When pitching progressive autonomy to PMs, the Cagan parallel makes the pattern instantly familiar.
4. **Resist the temptation to over-extend.** The mapping holds at the structural level; specific tactics may not transfer. A leadership tool for delegating outcomes is not always a UI for delegating AI actions — but the underlying loop is.

## Examples
1. **Suzy Decision Engine's enterprise rollout.** The same buyer who reads Cagan internally — "give your teams outcomes, build trust through observed delivery" — is the same buyer who needs progressive autonomy externally. Selling the pattern was easier when framed as "Cagan's empowerment loop, applied to your AI."
2. **Context Layer Demo's positioning.** When demoing to PM-led buyers, Riché frames the autonomy meter explicitly as "the trust-building pattern, but for your AI." Recognition is instant.

## Anti-patterns
- **Building AI autonomy without consulting product-strategy frameworks.** The team rebuilds the empowerment ladder from scratch and gets the dynamics wrong.
- **Treating the mapping as superficial.** Dropping "Cagan's framework" in a deck without operationalizing the loop. The label is not the work.

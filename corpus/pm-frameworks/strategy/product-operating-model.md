# Product Operating Model (Cagan)

## What it is

Marty Cagan's codified system of 20 principles describing how high-performing product organizations operate. The four core principles:

1. **Empowerment:** product teams are given **problems to solve, not features to ship**. They have autonomy to discover the right solution within strategic constraints.
2. **Outcomes over output:** teams are measured on the customer/business outcomes they produce, not the features they ship.
3. **Sense of ownership:** team members feel personally accountable for the outcome. Engineers care whether customers actually use what they built.
4. **Collaboration:** PM, designer, and tech lead operate as a "product trio," doing discovery and delivery together, not in handoff.

Around these four sit 16 supporting principles covering team structure (cross-functional, durable), strategic context (vision, strategy, OKRs), discovery practice (continuous, customer-driven), delivery practice (test/deploy as concurrent tracks), leadership (servant), recruiting (high bar), and culture (learning).

From Cagan's *Inspired* (2017), *Empowered* (2020), and *Transformed* (2024); SVPG's two-decade practice.

## Why it matters

The Product Operating Model is the system within which all the other Cagan tools (empowered teams, product vision/strategy, discovery/delivery tracks, trust barrier) cohere. Adopting any one in isolation usually fails because they reinforce each other; missing any one leaves a gap that breaks the others.

Most "agile transformations" or "product organization redesigns" fail because they pick three or four of the principles to import, leaving the rest. Empowered teams without product vision drift; outcomes-over-output without strategy is anarchic; ownership without collaboration produces silos. The 20 principles are a system, not a menu.

For AI products, the model is more relevant. AI's velocity rewards organizations that can run continuous discovery and delivery as one motion (not waterfall). The empowerment principle becomes existential — feature-team AI shops produce capability demos that don't compound. Outcomes-over-output is the only way to discriminate "AI features that move metrics" from "AI features that look impressive."

## How to apply

1. **Diagnose your current state against the 20 principles.** For each, score: present / partial / absent. The biggest gaps are usually leadership (insufficient strategic context) and ownership (incentives misaligned with outcomes).
2. **Sequence the transformation.** Cagan's order in *Transformed*: leadership and strategic context first, then team structure (durable, cross-functional, empowered), then practice (discovery/delivery, outcomes), then culture.
3. **Provide strategic context relentlessly.** Vision (years), strategy (quarterly), OKRs (quarterly). "Context replaces control" — without it, empowerment becomes chaos.
4. **Earn trust before granting more autonomy.** See `trust-barrier.md`. Trust compounds; impatient empowerment without trust regresses.
5. **Audit annually.** The 20 principles drift. Without explicit re-checking, organizations slide back to feature-team operations.

## Examples

1. **Suzy Decision Engine adoption.** Riché's transition of the Insights pillar from feature-team operation to empowered-team operation took roughly four quarters. Sequence: leadership alignment on strategic context (quarter 1), team restructuring into durable cross-functional trios (quarter 2), discovery practice introduction (quarter 3), outcomes-based accountability replacing feature-list scorecards (quarter 4). The model required all four; doing only two would have failed.
2. **Industry anti-pattern.** A SaaS company adopts "empowered teams" by renaming PMs "product managers" (they were "product owners" before) and keeping everything else identical: same feature roadmap, same output-based reviews, same waterfall handoffs. Nothing changes; the executive concludes empowerment doesn't work; the diagnosis is wrong.

## Anti-patterns

- **Cherry-picking principles.** Adopting outcomes-over-output without empowerment, or empowered teams without strategic context. The principles reinforce each other; partial adoption fails predictably.
- **Renaming-as-transformation.** Changing titles ("product owner" → "product manager"), org-chart redrawing, or new ceremony names while leaving the operating model untouched. Cagan's model is a behavior change, not a vocabulary change.

# Arc Step 3 — Introduce the Alternative

## What it is
Step 3 of the context generation story arc: present the five-step process that converts data into context. Curate, synthesize, consolidate, prioritize, store intelligently. This is the load-bearing center of the piece — every Wednesday deep dive contains this section, and it's the framework that travels into Thursday Frameworks posts and conference talks. The five steps are canonical. Don't substitute new verbs.

## Why it matters
By step 3, the reader knows the failure (step 1) and recognizes the assumption (step 2). They're now ready for an alternative — but only if it's specific enough to be tested. The five-step process gives them a vocabulary they can take back to their team. Without this step, the post is a critique. With it, the post is a tool.

## How to apply
1. Name the framework explicitly: "the five-step context generation process."
2. Walk through each step in order. Each gets one short paragraph or one sentence with a concrete example.
3. **Curate** — decide what data is in scope. Most teams skip this and pay for it later.
4. **Synthesize** — connect related signals into composites that answer questions, not just store facts.
5. **Consolidate** — collapse redundant signals. Five paragraphs saying the same thing become one.
6. **Prioritize** — rank what survives by decision-readiness. Would an expert use this to decide?
7. **Store intelligently** — index by query intent, not document boundary. The retrieval shape matches the question shape.
8. Use one named example per step where possible (Grandstage's curation rules, Suzy's consolidation pass, etc.).
9. Length: 250-350 words. This is the densest section of the piece.

## Examples

**Compressed version for a Tuesday Signal:** "The fix isn't bigger windows. It's a five-step pipeline: curate (decide what's in scope), synthesize (connect related signals), consolidate (collapse redundancy), prioritize (rank by decision-readiness), store intelligently (index by query intent). Every step Grandstage skipped, we paid for. Every step we added, accuracy went up."

**Mid-length version for a Wednesday Deep Dive (excerpt):** "Curate: at Grandstage we kept 12% of what we ingested. Synthesize: 30 raw signals collapse into 4 composite views per company. Consolidate: a second pass de-duplicates within composites — same earnings number cited five ways becomes one. Prioritize: a decision-readiness score keeps only what an analyst would use to write the brief. Store intelligently: the storage layer is shaped by the seven question types analysts actually ask, not the documents the data came in."

**Thursday Framework version:** A whole post can sit at step 3, expanded with a specific case study for each of the five steps.

## Anti-patterns
- Substituting verbs. "Filter, summarize, dedupe, rank, save" loses the canonical framing. Stick to curate, synthesize, consolidate, prioritize, store intelligently.
- Skipping steps to fit a tighter post. If a post can only fit two of the five, it's a hot take, not a deep dive — call it that.
- Steps without examples. Each step needs at least one concrete instance to be testable.

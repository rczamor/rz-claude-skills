# Hook Rotation — Weekly

## What it is
A scheduling rule for the five hook one-liners: each week, only one hook is the primary anchor across the week's content; the others get used in rotation and never repeat in adjacent weeks. The five canonical one-liners are: "Data is not context," "Retrieval without consolidation is just efficient delivery of noise," "Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem," "Expert decision-makers use less information, not more. Your AI should too," and "Models get smarter every month. Context quality stays the same."

## Why it matters
A great one-liner becomes a verbal tic if used every week. The voice depends on these lines feeling earned, not chanted. Rotation also distributes the surface area: different one-liners hook different readers (the Anthropic-release crowd vs. the cognitive-science crowd vs. the model-anxious crowd). And it gives Riché a built-in editorial calendar — knowing this week's anchor decides which content type each one-liner attaches to.

## How to apply
1. Pick the week's anchor on Sunday during the batch-writing block. The anchor depends on what's happening in the news cycle and which hook hasn't been used recently.
2. The anchor goes in the Wednesday Deep Dive headline or first sentence.
3. Two of the four other one-liners appear elsewhere in the week — one in Monday Hot Take, one in Tuesday Signal or Thursday Framework.
4. The remaining two one-liners go unused that week. They wait their turn.
5. No one-liner appears twice in the same week. No one-liner anchors two consecutive weeks.

## Examples

**Anchor week: "Data is not context."** Wednesday Deep Dive headline: "Your data isn't your context. Here's the five-step process that turns one into the other." Monday Hot Take closes with: "Models get smarter every month. Context quality stays the same. Which one are you working on?" Tuesday Signal uses "Retrieval without consolidation is just efficient delivery of noise" as a punch line on a vector-database announcement.

**Anchor week: "Expert decision-makers use less information, not more."** Wednesday Deep Dive opens with the Klein RPDM finding and grounds the prioritization step in it. Monday Hot Take: "If your prompt is 50 documents long, you're building a system that thinks like a novice." Friday Story uses "Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem." as the line a stakeholder said in a meeting that triggered the rebuild.

**Off-rotation:** A week where Riché publishes one Friday Story only (e.g., a personal post about IBM). No anchor needed; rotation pauses.

## Anti-patterns
- Same hook every week. Even the strongest line ("data is not context") wears out if it opens every Monday.
- Multiple hooks in one piece. A Tuesday Signal that uses three of the five in the same 350 words feels like a slogan parade.
- Skipping the rotation log. Without tracking which weeks used which anchor, the feed drifts back to repeating the favorite one. Keep a one-line note in the content tracker.

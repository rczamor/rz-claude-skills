# Batch Writing Workflow

## What it is
A 2-hour Sunday writing block that produces all 3-5 LinkedIn posts for the upcoming week. One sitting, one rhythm, one voice. The batch eliminates mid-week improvisation — which is when voice drifts, the Fatal Fifteen sneak in, and posts get drafted at 11pm on a Tuesday with the wrong tone. Sunday batch writing is the load-bearing weekly ritual of the entire content system.

## Why it matters
Without batching, content production has to happen between meetings on weekdays — which is exactly when Riché has the least cognitive headroom for voice work. Batched, the same five posts take half the time and read consistently. The Sunday block also forces editorial decisions in advance: which hot take, which signal, which deep dive, which framework, which story. By Tuesday the work is execution and engagement, not drafting. The 5.25-hour weekly budget assumes batch writing; without it, the budget breaks.

## How to apply

1. **Sunday block, 2 hours, no interruptions.** Phone in another room. One coffee, one playlist, one document open.

2. **Open with the rotation log.** Which hook anchored last week? Which hooks haven't fired in a while? Pick this week's anchor before drafting.

3. **Order of drafting (in order of decreasing energy needed):**
   - Wednesday Deep Dive first (longest, requires most thinking).
   - Thursday Framework second (structured, takes less time).
   - Tuesday Signal third (depends on the news cycle, so often half-drafted with placeholder for the trigger).
   - Monday Hot Take fourth (can come from a leftover paragraph in the deep dive).
   - Friday Story fifth (most personal; benefits from being last when the week's voice is warmest).

4. **One pass per post.** Don't perfect; complete. The Fatal Fifteen check happens at the publish gate (see `workflow-fatal-fifteen-gate.md`), not during drafting.

5. **Schedule into the content tracker.** Each post gets a publish date, a content type tag, and a hook used.

6. **Tuesday Signal placeholder.** Since signals depend on actual news, Sunday drafts a structural template and waits for the trigger event Monday/Tuesday.

## Examples

**Sample Sunday output:**
- Wednesday: 950-word deep dive on the Suzy Decision Engine launch, anchor hook "Data is not context."
- Thursday: 550-word framework post on the Decision-Readiness Test, supporting hook "Expert decision-makers use less information, not more."
- Tuesday: signal placeholder — structure ready, trigger TBD.
- Monday: 200-word hot take pulled from a paragraph cut from the deep dive, supporting hook "Retrieval without consolidation is just efficient delivery of noise."
- Friday: 550-word story on the IBM personalization platform decision that taught Riché the difference between curating and ingesting.

**What goes wrong without batching.** A typical pre-batch week: Monday draft at 9pm, rushed; Tuesday signal late by a day; Wednesday deep dive abandoned mid-week because of a sprint planning conflict; Thursday and Friday skipped entirely. Net: 1-2 posts/week, voice inconsistent, hook rotation broken.

**Batch deferral.** When a Sunday is unavailable (travel, family), batch shifts to the previous Friday evening. Never to Monday morning — that compresses the week.

## Anti-patterns
- Mid-week drafting. The biggest single cause of voice drift. If Wednesday's deep dive is being drafted on Tuesday night, the post will read as such.
- Editing during drafting. Switching between writing mode and editing mode in the same block doubles the time. Edit only at the publish gate.
- Skipping the rotation log. Without tracking which hooks fired which week, the feed reverts to the favorite hook on repeat.

# Hook Use in Hot Takes — Single One-Liner

## What it is
A rule for how Monday Hot Takes use the hook library: hot takes use one supporting one-liner only — never the full anchor "Data is not context." Hot takes are 150-250 words. The one-liner is the entire spine of the post. There's no room for both an anchor and a supporting line; trying to fit both turns a hot take into a stub of a deep dive.

## Why it matters
The anchor "Data is not context" is the heaviest line in the inventory. It carries the most weight, and when used, it expects 700-1,000 words to back it up. Hot takes can't deliver that backup. They use the lighter, more specific one-liners that can stand alone with 200 words of evidence: "Retrieval without consolidation is just efficient delivery of noise" or "Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem." Both can land in a single short post.

## How to apply
1. Pick one supporting one-liner from the rotation. Skip the anchor.
2. The one-liner is the first sentence of the hot take, or the last. Sometimes both — opening hook, closing callback — but only when the post is exactly that one claim, said twice with a beat in between.
3. The body of the hot take is one or two short paragraphs of evidence. Specific moment, decision, metric, or named tool.
4. No second hook. No "and here's another way to say it." One claim, one beat, one proof.
5. If the hot take feels like it needs a second hook, it's not a hot take — it's an under-developed deep dive. Either expand or rewrite.

## Examples

**Single one-liner, opening:** "Retrieval without consolidation is just efficient delivery of noise. Grandstage v1 retrieved 50 docs and got the wrong answer. Grandstage v2 retrieved 4 and shipped. Same data, different system. The model didn't change. The pipeline did. From 50 to 4. Better answer."

**Single one-liner, opening + light callback:** "Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem. I've watched two teams in the last quarter swap models hoping it would fix what was upstream of the model. Both teams burned six weeks. Both ended up rewriting the context layer. Stop changing models. Start changing what reaches the model."

**Single one-liner, closing:** A short observation about a competitor's recent launch closes with "Models get smarter every month. Context quality stays the same. Pick one to work on this quarter." The hook is the period at the end of the post.

## Anti-patterns
- Anchor in a hot take. "Data is not context. Here's why in 200 words." The anchor expects a deep dive backing. The hot take can't deliver it. The line falls flat.
- Two one-liners in 200 words. They cancel each other out — the reader hears slogans, not a position.
- One-liner with no proof. Even in 200 words, the hot take needs one specific number, named tool, or moment. Otherwise it's provocation, not thought.

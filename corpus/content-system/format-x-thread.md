# X Thread Format

## What it is
The X (Twitter) cross-posting format: shorter, punchier, thread-native. X content is repurposed from LinkedIn rather than written fresh — the source material is the same week's posts, adapted for X's tighter format and its different audience (Segment 3 founders, engineers, and AI-native operators rather than enterprise PM peers). Threads are used for deep dives; standalone posts handle hot takes and signals.

## Why it matters
X is the secondary channel, not the primary. Trying to write fresh content for X violates the 5.25-hour weekly budget. But ignoring X means losing reach into Segment 3 (founders building AI products) and into the founder/engineer audience that lives there. The right balance: every LinkedIn post gets adapted for X within 24 hours, and the adaptation takes 5-10 minutes per post, not 30.

## How to apply

1. **Hot take → standalone X post.** A 200-word LinkedIn hot take becomes a 280-character X post. Compress to the hook + the strongest sentence. Cut everything else.

2. **Signal → quote tweet with added perspective.** When a Tuesday Signal reacts to news, the X version is a quote tweet of the original news with one or two paragraphs of Riché's lens. Adds value rather than just amplifying.

3. **Deep dive → 5-7 tweet thread.** Each tweet is one beat of the six-step story arc. Numbered 1/, 2/, etc. Last tweet links back to the LinkedIn article.

4. **Framework → 4-6 tweet thread.** First tweet states the framework name and what problem it solves. Each subsequent tweet covers one component. Last tweet has a real example.

5. **Story → standalone post or short thread.** Friday Stories work natively on X if compressed; longer ones become 3-tweet threads.

6. **Engagement targets:** quote tweet (with added perspective) the X-native voices in the network — @shreyaborhade, @lennysan, @joulee, @johncutlefish, @sachinrekhi, @caborojo, @gibsonbiddle, @lissijean, @ttorres.

## Examples

**Hot take adapted for X:**
LinkedIn (210 words): "Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem. I've watched two teams swap models..."
X (240 chars): "Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem. I've watched two teams swap models in the last quarter hoping it would fix what was upstream. Both burned six weeks. Both ended up rewriting context. Stop changing models."

**Deep dive thread structure:**
1/ Three weeks after launch, our top user told me Grandstage was lying to her.
2/ The retrieval pulled the right blog post, the right earnings call, the right LinkedIn post. The summary cited a number we'd seen nowhere.
3/ The model wasn't broken. The context was. Most teams assume data = context. Here's the five-step pipeline that converts one to the other.
4/ Curate. Synthesize. Consolidate. Prioritize. Store intelligently. [one line each in tweets 4-5]
5/ ...
6/ Three quarters later: 300% growth, $0 CAC.
7/ Full piece on LinkedIn → [link]

**Framework thread.** A Decision-Readiness Test thread compresses the Thursday Framework's 500 words into 6 tweets, with the Suzy 4x reduction as the closing example.

## Anti-patterns
- Writing fresh content for X. Burns time, breaks the budget, and almost always underperforms the LinkedIn original.
- Bare retweets. Quote with added perspective; never just amplify without a take.
- Thread-as-LinkedIn-post. Pasting a long LinkedIn post into a single X tweet ignores the format. Threads exist for a reason.

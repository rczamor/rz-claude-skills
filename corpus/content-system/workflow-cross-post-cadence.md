# Cross-Post Cadence Workflow

## What it is
The schedule for publishing each piece across LinkedIn (primary) and X (secondary): LinkedIn first, X next-day with thread-native rework. Never simultaneous. Never reverse order. The cadence preserves LinkedIn's algorithmic priority (the post compounds in the LinkedIn feed for the first 24 hours), allows the X version to be properly adapted instead of pasted, and respects the 5.25-hour weekly budget by keeping cross-posting to 5-10 minutes per piece.

## Why it matters
Simultaneous cross-posting hurts both surfaces. LinkedIn's algorithm reads cross-posted content as low-effort and dampens reach. X readers can tell when a post is a LinkedIn paste-job and don't engage. Sequential cross-posting — with the X version genuinely rewritten in thread format or tightened for character count — solves both problems. It also creates a "second wave" of attention 24 hours after the LinkedIn drop, when the LinkedIn engagement is peaking and the X follow-up amplifies it.

## How to apply

1. **Day-of (LinkedIn primary).** Publish the post on LinkedIn at the time the audience is most active — typically 8-10am ET on weekdays for Segment 2. Spend 15-20 minutes engaging in comments in the first hour after publishing.

2. **Day +1 (X cross-post).** Within 24 hours, publish the X version. Adapted, not pasted.

3. **Adaptation rules by content type:**
   - Hot Take: 280-character single tweet, hook only, drop everything else.
   - Signal: quote tweet of the news with one tight paragraph of Riché's lens.
   - Deep Dive: 5-7 tweet thread, each tweet a beat of the story arc, last tweet links to the LinkedIn article.
   - Framework: 4-6 tweet thread, first tweet states the framework, subsequent tweets cover components, last tweet has a real example.
   - Story: 280-char single post or 3-tweet thread.

4. **Engagement after X cross-post.** 5-10 minutes engaging with X-native voices (founders, engineers) in the comments and via quote-tweet of related discussions.

5. **Track the second wave.** When the LinkedIn post is performing well, the X post often catches a different audience — Segment 3 founders. Note which kind of cross-post earns the second wave; use that signal in the next week's batch.

## Examples

**Sequence for a Wednesday Deep Dive on Grandstage:**
- Wed 9am ET: LinkedIn Article published. Engage in comments for 20 min.
- Wed 9:30am-end-of-day: Engagement-replies as comments come in.
- Thu 10am ET: X thread (6 tweets) published. Quote-tweet @joulee's recent thread on retrieval architecture as a side conversation. Engage 10 min.

**Sequence for a Monday Hot Take:**
- Mon 8am ET: LinkedIn post published. Engage in comments 15 min.
- Tue 9am ET: X version (single tweet, 240 chars) published. 5 min engagement.

**Why simultaneous fails.** Imagine the same Wednesday Deep Dive published at 9am simultaneously to LinkedIn and X. LinkedIn algorithm sees a long-form post that exists elsewhere and dampens reach. X readers see a paste-job thread that doesn't read natively and don't engage. Both surfaces underperform compared to the sequential cadence.

**Schedule reset for travel/holidays.** If LinkedIn slot is missed (travel, family), defer the X cross-post by the same number of days. Never publish to X first to fill the gap; that breaks the cadence and confuses the audience that follows both.

## Anti-patterns
- Simultaneous cross-posting. Hurts both surfaces.
- Paste-job X posts. If the X version is a paste, skip it — don't damage the X presence with low-effort content.
- Reverse cadence (X first, LinkedIn second). Breaks LinkedIn's algorithmic preference for first-publish content.

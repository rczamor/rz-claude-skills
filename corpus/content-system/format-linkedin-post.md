# LinkedIn Post Format

## What it is
The primary publishing format for Riché's thought leadership: 3-5 LinkedIn posts per week, each tailored to its weekday content type (Mon/Tue/Wed/Thu/Fri). LinkedIn is the channel where Segments 1-3 of the audience overlap and where the content flywheel turns fastest. The format constraints — line breaks for mobile readability, hook-first openings, no link in the first comment for short posts, articles for deep dives — exist to match how the LinkedIn feed actually works in 2025-2026.

## Why it matters
LinkedIn is where the content strategy starts. X is downstream; the newsletter is deferred to Q3 2026. If LinkedIn isn't working, nothing is. The format choices on LinkedIn determine how many people see the post (the hook decides the dwell time, the dwell time decides the algorithmic distribution) and what kind of engagement it earns (substantive comments from senior PMs vs. emoji reactions from strangers).

## How to apply

1. **Length by content type.**
   - Hot Take: 150-250 words.
   - Signal: 200-400 words.
   - Deep Dive: publish as a LinkedIn Article (700-1,000 words) or a long post.
   - Framework: 400-700 words.
   - Story: variable, usually 350-700 words.

2. **Mobile formatting.** Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences). Empty line between paragraphs. The first three lines must hook before the "see more" cutoff.

3. **Hook in the first 100 characters.** This is the line that runs across the feed before the truncation. Every hook one-liner is built for this constraint.

4. **No "Click here for the link" in the post body.** Links go in the first comment for posts; in the article body for articles.

5. **Tag people only when genuinely referencing their work.** Tagging Lenny, Shreyas, or Melissa to fish for a reshare backfires.

6. **Engage in comments before posting and within the first hour after.** The algorithm rewards active accounts; posting and walking away kills the post.

## Examples

**Mobile-friendly hot take opening (first three lines):**
```
Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem.

I've watched two teams swap models in the last quarter hoping it would fix what was upstream of the model.

Both teams burned six weeks.
```
The "see more" hits after the first paragraph. The reader has already committed.

**Article publishing for a Wednesday Deep Dive.** A 900-word piece on the Grandstage rebuild gets published as a LinkedIn Article rather than a long post — Articles index for SEO via LinkedIn's domain authority and provide a permanent shareable URL. The first comment is left for a related-reading link or a question that invites comments.

**Tagging done right.** A Tuesday Signal that extends a Lenny essay tags @LennyRachitsky in the body where the reference is — not in the post intro to fish for amplification.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating LinkedIn like a blog. Long unbroken paragraphs die on mobile.
- Ignoring the comment hour. Posting and disappearing tells the algorithm the post isn't interesting. Spend 15 minutes engaging in your own comments.
- Posting without an audience target. Every post should be aimed at one of Segments 1-3 — see `corpus/growth/audience-segments.md`.

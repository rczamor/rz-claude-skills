# CFP Submission Format

## What it is
The complete packet submitted to a conference Call for Proposals. Five components: title, abstract, takeaways (bullet list), bio, and "why now" / "why me" rationale. CFP packets are how Riché reaches Segment 4 (event organizers). Strong CFPs that name real systems and signal live demo capability outperform abstract-only submissions because organizers are evaluating the speaker, not just the topic.

## Why it matters
Event organizers receive 50-200 CFP submissions for ~10 slots. The packet has to do three jobs in 60 seconds of skim time: prove the topic is timely, prove the speaker can deliver, and signal that the talk will be memorable (often the live demo angle). A great talk concept loses to a weaker concept with a tighter packet. The packet also gives organizers reusable language for their own marketing — they pull from the title, abstract, and takeaways when promoting the event.

## How to apply

The full packet has five components.

1. **Title (under 80 characters).** A clear, specific phrase that earns a click on the program page. "Data Is Not Context: The Five-Step Pipeline Behind AI Products That Actually Decide" beats "Modern Context Architecture for AI." Avoid colon-overload titles ("Beyond RAG: Reimagining: ..."); one colon maximum.

2. **Abstract (200-300 words).** Problem → thesis → takeaway → demo signal. See `format-talk-abstract.md` for the full structure.

3. **Three to five takeaways (bullet list).** Each starts with a verb. Each is concrete enough to test. Examples: "Run a five-step audit on any AI product in under a week." "Apply Klein's recognition-primed decision making to context prioritization." "Use the Decision-Readiness Test to cut context size 4x without quality loss."

4. **Bio (75-150 words).** See `format-speaker-bio.md`. Use the standard 100-word version unless the CFP specifies otherwise.

5. **Why now / why me (100-150 words).** Two paragraphs. Why now: the AI moment makes context architecture the bottleneck of the next 12 months. Why me: the named systems and the live demo capability. This section is where Segment 4 makes the decision.

6. **Optional fields.** Some CFPs ask for video samples, prior speaking, intended audience. Always tailor: prior speaking is a build-up that benefits from being present even when minimal; intended audience signals fit (e.g., "senior PMs and AI product leaders" matches Segment 2).

## Examples

**Title options (pick one):**
- "Data Is Not Context: The Five-Step Pipeline Behind AI Products That Actually Decide"
- "The Real Moat in AI: Context Architecture, Not Model Intelligence"
- "Why Your Best AI Product Has a Context Problem (and How to Fix It in Six Weeks)"

**Takeaway list example:**
- Run the five-step context audit (curate, synthesize, consolidate, prioritize, store intelligently) on your AI product within a week.
- Apply Klein's recognition-primed decision making to your prioritization layer to mirror expert decision behavior.
- Use the Decision-Readiness Test to cut prompt context size 4x without quality loss.
- Predict whether your next model upgrade will fix the problem you think it will.
- Use the Grandstage / Suzy / Context Layer Engine architectures as reference patterns for your own roadmap.

**Why now / why me example:**
"Why now: the gap between model capability and product capability has never been wider. Models double in capability every six months. Most AI products improve in inverse proportion. The bottleneck has shifted from model intelligence to context quality, and there's no playbook yet for what to do about it.

Why me: I've shipped context systems behind 300% user growth at Grandstage, a Decision Engine at Suzy that moved 350+ enterprise customers in six weeks, and personalization platforms at IBM generating millions across product lines. I'm currently building the Context Layer Engine — a personal knowledge system that runs the architecture I'll be teaching. I can demo it live on stage so the audience sees the architecture running, not just diagrammed."

## Anti-patterns
- Generic CFP packet. The same packet sent to every conference reads as low-effort. Tailor the why-now and the title to the event.
- Missing the live demo signal. For events with a strong builder audience, the demo capability is the differentiator. Leaving it out cedes the advantage.
- Takeaways that are vague. "Understand context architecture better" is not a takeaway. "Run a five-step audit on your AI product within a week" is.

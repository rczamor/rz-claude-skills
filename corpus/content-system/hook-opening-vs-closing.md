# Hook Opening vs. Closing

## What it is
A placement rule: hooks open pieces; proof points close pieces. Don't swap them. The hook is the line that interrupts the scroll; the proof point is the line that ends with the reader believing it. Opening with a number — "300% user growth" — buries the lede; closing with a hook — "data is not context" — lets the air out of the piece.

## Why it matters
The order matters because attention works in a specific shape: readers commit in the first sentence and decide what to do with what they read in the last sentence. The hook earns the commitment. The proof earns the conclusion. Reversing them flattens the arc — the reader skims through a number they can't yet evaluate, then closes on a slogan with no fuel.

## How to apply
1. Open with a hook one-liner or a failure scene (which is the narrative version of a hook).
2. Save proof points for the body and especially the close. The strongest specific number gets the last line.
3. If a piece must lead with a number (rare — usually a Tuesday Signal reacting to a metric in the news), the number is the hook. Don't add another hook above it.
4. The close is the proof point that locks in the position the hook opened. They should rhyme thematically.
5. In short pieces (Monday Hot Takes), the hook can be the first line and the proof the last line, with one sentence in between.

## Examples

**Correct ordering — Monday Hot Take:** "Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem. [body: two short paragraphs]. At Grandstage, the model never changed. The context did. Growth went from flat to 300%."

**Correct ordering — Wednesday Deep Dive:** Opens with a failure scene at Grandstage. Closes with: "Three quarters later, $0 CAC, 300% growth, every new user came from another user. The architecture is what kept the product's word at scale."

**Incorrect — opening with proof:** "300% user growth at Grandstage. Here's how I think about context architecture..." The number lands before the reader cares about it. Better: "Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem." Then earn the 300% over 600 words.

**Incorrect — closing with hook:** Ends a 900-word deep dive with "Data is not context." The reader has already read 900 words establishing this. Restating the hook at the end deflates the piece. Close on the specific number or the next problem.

## Anti-patterns
- Hook → hook → hook. Three one-liners stacked back-to-back is a slogan parade. Pick one to open and earn it.
- Proof → proof → proof. Three numbers in a row reads like a portfolio bullet list. The numbers need narrative connective tissue.
- Closing on a "key takeaway." Faux-summary closings are in the Fatal Fifteen. Close on the proof or on the next question.

# Speaker Bio Format

## What it is
A 75-150 word speaker bio that positions Riché practitioner-first, names two or three real systems he's built, and signals the live demo capability. Used in CFP submissions, podcast pitch packets, event programs, profile pages, and anywhere an event organizer needs a paragraph to introduce him. Not a resume; not a tagline. A density-optimized paragraph that earns the speaking slot.

## Why it matters
Event organizers and podcast hosts skim ten bios in a row. Bios that read like resumes ("PM at Suzy, formerly IBM, formerly Helm Labs") get filed without a second read. Bios that lead with a position ("Riché Zamor builds context architecture for AI products...") and back it with named systems get shortlisted. The bio is also where Segment 4 (event organizers) makes their decision — without a bio that signals practitioner credibility and a live demo hook, the submission stalls regardless of the abstract quality.

## How to apply

1. **Sentence 1 — the position.** One sentence stating what Riché does, not where he works. "Riché Zamor builds context architecture for AI products — the layer between raw data and the LLM that decides what an agent actually knows."

2. **Sentence 2 — the proof.** Two or three named systems with one number each. "He's shipped context systems behind 300% user growth at Grandstage, a Decision Engine launch at Suzy that moved 350+ enterprise customers in six weeks, and personalization platforms at IBM generating millions across product lines."

3. **Sentence 3 — the differentiator.** What makes him a practitioner, not a commentator. Often the live demo line. "He's built a personal knowledge engine to demonstrate the architecture live on stage."

4. **Sentence 4 (optional) — the lens.** Where he draws from. "His work translates cognitive science into product architecture: how expert decision-makers use less information, not more, and what that means for AI."

5. **Length:** 75-150 words. Closer to 100 for most events; closer to 150 only when the event explicitly requests longer.

## Examples

**Standard 100-word bio:**
"Riché Zamor builds context architecture for AI products — the layer between raw data and the LLM that decides what an agent actually knows. He's shipped context systems behind 300% user growth at Grandstage, a Decision Engine launch at Suzy that moved 350+ enterprise customers in six weeks, and personalization platforms at IBM generating millions in revenue. He's built a personal knowledge engine, the Context Layer Engine, to demonstrate the architecture live on stage. His work translates cognitive science into product architecture — how expert decision-makers use less information, not more, and what that means for AI."

**Short 75-word bio (for tight programs):**
"Riché Zamor builds context architecture for AI products. He's shipped context systems behind 300% user growth at Grandstage and a Decision Engine launch at Suzy that moved 350+ enterprise customers in six weeks. He builds a personal knowledge engine, the Context Layer Engine, to demonstrate the architecture live on stage. His work translates cognitive science — how experts use less information, not more — into product decisions."

**Why a full 150-word version exists.** For events that ask for full bios with affiliations, prior speaking, and one personal note, expand the standard with one sentence on prior conferences and one personal note (e.g., based in NYC, advisor to two seed-stage AI startups).

## Anti-patterns
- Resume-style bio. "Currently PM at X, previously at Y, before that Z." Boring, low-credibility, fileable.
- Vague claims. "Has worked extensively in AI product development." Useless. Specific systems and numbers only.
- Missing the demo hook. The live demo capability is a primary differentiator for Segment 4 — leaving it out cedes the advantage.

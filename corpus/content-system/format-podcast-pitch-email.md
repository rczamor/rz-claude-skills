# Podcast Pitch Email Format

## What it is
A short, specific email pitching Riché as a podcast guest. Four moves in ~150-250 words: hook, specific angle, proof point, call to action. The pitch gets read in 30 seconds by a busy podcast host or producer. Sent to Lenny Rachitsky, Melissa Perri, Shreyas Doshi, the SVPG / Product Therapy team, All Things Product, the Product Podcast — anywhere Segment 4 (podcast hosts) lives.

## Why it matters
Podcast appearances are the highest-leverage content move available within the 5.25-hour weekly budget. One Lenny appearance reaches more of Segment 1 (hiring managers) than three months of LinkedIn posts. But podcast pitches drown — hosts get hundreds of pitches a quarter, and the generic "I'd love to come on your podcast to talk about AI" never gets a reply. The format constraints exist to break through that noise: specific show familiarity, specific angle, specific proof, specific ask.

## How to apply

The email has four moves.

1. **Hook + show familiarity (1-2 sentences).** Reference a specific recent episode by name and the moment that resonated. Not "I love your show." Specific. "Your conversation with [guest] on [date] about [topic] — particularly the moment when they said [quote] — is exactly the failure mode I've been writing about."

2. **The specific angle (2-3 sentences).** What you'd talk about, framed as a contrarian or additive take. Use the canonical hook. "I'd love to come on to argue that the AI product moat isn't model intelligence — it's context architecture. Models get smarter every month. Context quality stays the same. Most teams are working on the wrong problem."

3. **One proof point (1-2 sentences).** The named systems and a number. "I've shipped context systems behind 300% user growth at Grandstage and a Decision Engine launch at Suzy that moved 350+ enterprise customers in six weeks. I built the Context Layer Engine to demonstrate the architecture, and I can walk through it live on the show."

4. **Call to action (1 sentence).** Specific, low-friction. "Happy to send a one-page outline if useful, or jump on a 15-minute call if that's easier."

5. **Length:** 150-250 words. Subject line follows the same hook rules as LinkedIn opening lines.

## Examples

**Subject line options:**
- "The AI moat isn't models — it's context (a possible episode)"
- "Context architecture pitch — 200 words"
- "Pitch: data is not context (and why your favorite agent is bad)"

**Sample pitch (210 words):**

Subject: The AI moat isn't models — it's context (a possible episode)

"Hi [Host],

Your conversation with [guest] last month on [specific topic] kept coming back to a tension I think is the central one in AI product right now: the model is getting smarter, the agent is getting worse. The episode named the symptom; I've been working on the diagnosis.

I'd love to come on to argue that the AI product moat isn't model intelligence — it's context architecture. The thesis: data is not context. Most teams pipe raw data into an LLM, call it RAG, and ship an agent that gets smarter with every model release and stays equally bad at deciding what matters. The fix is a five-step pipeline upstream of the model: curate, synthesize, consolidate, prioritize, store intelligently.

I've shipped context systems behind 300% user growth at Grandstage and a Decision Engine launch at Suzy that moved 350+ enterprise customers in six weeks. I'm currently building the Context Layer Engine — a personal knowledge system that runs the architecture — and I can walk through it live on the show.

Happy to send a one-page outline if useful, or jump on a 15-minute call if that's easier.

Best,
Riché"

## Anti-patterns
- Generic show familiarity. "I love your podcast." Reads as low-effort. Reference one specific episode.
- Three-paragraph windups. The host stops reading after 30 seconds. Lead with the angle.
- No proof point. Saying "I'd be a great guest" is not a credential. Named systems and numbers only.

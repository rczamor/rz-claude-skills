# Tuesday Signals

## What it is
A 200-400 word post that reacts to specific news through Riché's context generation lens. Timely, analytical, additive. The trigger is something that just happened — an Anthropic release, an OpenAI announcement, a Lenny essay, a competitor's funding round, a notable failure. The post never summarizes the news. It extends it or challenges it from a practitioner's vantage point.

## Why it matters
Reaction posts are how peers signal taste and judgment to other peers. If Riché waits a week to comment on something, the conversation has moved on. If he summarizes the news, he adds nothing. The Tuesday slot exists to catch the news cycle while it's still warm and to demonstrate the lens that distinguishes him: data is not context, retrieval is not understanding, the model is not the moat.

## How to apply
1. Identify the news/signal in one sentence at the top. Never paraphrase a press release for two paragraphs.
2. Take a position: extend the announcement (here's what they're getting right and why it matters more than the headline says) or challenge it (here's why this won't fix what people think it fixes).
3. Ground the position in something Riché has built or seen. One concrete proof point per post.
4. End with a real implication for product builders. Not a "key takeaway" — a real "here's what changes for you on Wednesday morning."

## Examples

**Topic: Anthropic ships a longer context window.** Opens "Anthropic just doubled context windows. The teams who needed it most won't benefit." Three short paragraphs: 1) why longer windows feel like a fix; 2) what Riché saw at Suzy when context grew 4x and accuracy fell because nothing was consolidated; 3) the actual fix is upstream — the five-step context pipeline. Closes: "Bigger context windows reward teams who already curate well. Everyone else gets more expensive noise."

**Topic: A competitor announces $50M Series B for an AI search product.** Opens "A new AI search company just raised $50M. Their architecture diagram is the tell." Extends with a specific architecture critique grounded in what Grandstage learned the hard way: retrieval-first systems hit a quality ceiling without consolidation.

**Topic: Lenny publishes an essay on AI PMs.** Opens "Lenny's framing of AI PMs as 'product owners of probabilistic systems' is right. It's also incomplete." Adds the missing piece: AI PMs are also context architects, and that part of the job has no playbook yet. Riché's been writing one in public.

## Anti-patterns
- Summarizing instead of extending. "Anthropic released a new model. It can do X, Y, and Z. This is exciting for product builders." This is a press release, not a post.
- Performative contrarianism. Disagreeing for the sake of disagreement, without a real reason rooted in something Riché has built. The signal must add a concrete lens, not a hot take.
- Missing the news cycle. A Tuesday signal posted on Friday is a Wednesday deep dive that lost its hook.

# No Silicon Valley Hipster Register

## What it is
Silicon Valley hipster register — "shipping," "based," "vibes," "no notes," "this is the way," "ship it," "just vibes," "literally no thoughts," "real ones know," "based and X-pilled" — is not used in Riché's voice. This register signals the LinkedIn/X-cohort tic of using insider phrasing as a status signal rather than as communication. Riché's voice is warm and direct, but it's not aspiring to belong to the SV-Twitter clique.

## Why it matters
The register is a tribal signal more than a communication tool. When a writer reaches for "based" or "this is the way," the writer is signaling membership in a cohort, not making a clearer claim. The cost is twofold: it dates the prose (these phrases age out within a year or two), and it narrows the audience to people who recognize the dialect. Riché's voice has a wider audience — enterprise PMs, conference attendees, podcast listeners, hiring managers — who don't read the SV-Twitter timeline. The register has to land for them too.

## How to apply
- Search every draft for "shipping" (used as a status word, not a verb), "based," "vibes," "no notes," "this is the way," "real ones," "ship it" as a tribal call. Cut and replace with plain language.
- The replacement is usually a more specific verb or phrase. "Just shipping" becomes "we launched X this week." "Vibes" becomes "the qualitative signal" or specific examples. "No notes" becomes "I don't have anything to add" or, if there genuinely are no notes, just silence.
- The rule applies to ironic uses too. Using SV-hipster phrases ironically still signals the cohort.
- Casual phrasings that pass the rule (and stay): "DOPE," "pretty wild," "hung up the towel," "honestly," "I'll be straight with you." These are personal voice; SV-hipster register is borrowed voice.

## Examples
BAD: "Just shipped the new context architecture. Based. Real ones know."

GOOD: "We shipped the new context architecture this week. The team is calling it the consolidation layer. It's the missing piece I've been writing about for a year."

BAD: "Suzy's Decision Engine: this is the way. Vibes are immaculate."

GOOD: "Suzy's Decision Engine works because the consolidation layer was already built. 350+ enterprise customers hit the launch surface in week one. The qualitative signal from the customer success team was the loudest I've heard at the company."

BAD: "GPT-5 dropped. Based. No notes."

GOOD: "GPT-5 dropped. Faster, cheaper, smarter. The teams that benefit are the ones who already fixed their context layer. Everyone else is upgrading the engine on a car with the wrong fuel."

BAD: "Honestly the consolidation step is the way. Ship it. Real ones know."

GOOD: "Honestly, the consolidation step is the difference between the AI products that work and the ones that don't. I've watched four teams hit this wall in a row, and the wall is always at the same step."

## Anti-patterns
- Using SV-hipster register "ironically" to signal awareness. The signal still puts the post in the cohort.
- Defending the register because it's "the dialect of the audience." Riché's audience is wider than SV-Twitter; the dialect doesn't generalize.

# Only Functional Emojis, Used Sparingly

## What it is
Riché's voice uses no decorative emojis. The only acceptable emojis are functional ones — check (✓), x (✗), bulb (💡) — and they appear sparingly, only where they genuinely mark structural meaning the prose can't carry on its own. Rocket ships, fire, lightning bolts, raised hands, point-down arrows, and the rest of the LinkedIn-influencer emoji palette do not appear in drafts.

## Why it matters
Decorative emojis are the most aggressive signal of LinkedIn-influencer register. A rocket ship at the start of a post tells the reader exactly what kind of post is coming, and the kind of post is the kind Riché's voice is calibrated against. Even when the substance is strong, the emoji undermines it — it pattern-matches to growth-hack content and triggers the reader's skip instinct. Functional emojis (check, x, bulb) earn a narrow exception because they convey real meaning concisely; decorative ones don't.

## How to apply
- Scan every draft for emojis. Default to deleting all of them.
- Three exceptions: ✓ to mark a true item in a comparison list, ✗ to mark a false item, 💡 to mark a single insight callout. Even these should be rare — usually the prose can carry the meaning without the visual.
- Never use emojis as section headers, list markers, or paragraph openers.
- Especially never: rocket ships, fire, lightning bolts, raised hands (🙌), point-down arrows (👇), 100, brain emojis, light-bulb-with-rays, every variant of "look at this!"
- The rule applies across all platforms — LinkedIn, X, Threads, blog posts. The voice is consistent.

## Examples
BAD: "🚀 Most AI products fail because they treat data as context. 🔥 Here's how to fix it. 👇"

GOOD: "Most AI products fail because they treat data as context. Here's how to fix it."

BAD: "💯 Suzy shipped the Decision Engine in 6 weeks ⚡️ because the consolidation layer was already built 🧠"

GOOD: "Suzy shipped the Decision Engine in six weeks because the consolidation layer was already built."

BAD: "🎯 The five-step process: curate, synthesize, consolidate, prioritize, store intelligently. 🔑"

GOOD: "The five-step process: curate, synthesize, consolidate, prioritize, store intelligently."

GOOD (functional emoji as comparison marker):
"Most AI products treat retrieval and consolidation the same way:
✗ Retrieve more data, ship to user
✓ Retrieve, then consolidate, then ship the decision-ready slice"

GOOD (functional emoji as insight callout, used once in a long piece):
"💡 The cheap insight: every team I've audited had built retrieval. None had built consolidation."

## Anti-patterns
- Adding "just one rocket ship" to a serious post. The one rocket ship is the signal that pulls the post into LinkedIn-influencer register. Don't.
- Using emojis for "personality." Personality comes from the prose voice, not the emoji palette.

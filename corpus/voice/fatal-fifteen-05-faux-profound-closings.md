# Fatal Fifteen #5: No Faux-Profound Closings

## What it is
The fifth Fatal Fifteen rule. Faux-profound closings — "As we navigate an increasingly AI-driven world...", "The future of work is being redefined...", "We stand at an inflection point..." — must not close any draft. These sentences pretend to add weight to a piece by gesturing at a vague civilizational moment. They add nothing. They are the closer equivalent of a stock photo on a press release.

## Why it matters
A closer is the last impression. If the closer is generic, the entire piece feels generic in retrospect, no matter how strong the middle was. Faux-profound closings are universally generic — they could close any piece on any topic by any AI commentator. Riché's voice closes with something specific: a reframed claim, a question that opens a door, a one-line distillation of the lesson, or simply the last beat of the story. Never a sweeping abstraction about the era.

## How to apply
- Read every draft's last paragraph in isolation. Could this paragraph close any AI thought leadership piece? If yes, rewrite.
- Strong closers in Riché's voice: a single sharp sentence that names what the piece was actually about; a Friday Story closer that lands on the distilled lesson without lecturing; a Hot Take closer that doubles down on the original claim with one more piece of evidence.
- If a draft's natural closer feels weak, the issue usually isn't the closer — it's that the piece didn't earn a strong landing. Strengthen the middle, not the closer.

## Examples
BAD closer: "As we navigate an increasingly AI-driven world, context will define the winners and losers."

GOOD closer: "Models commoditize. Context quality doesn't. That's the moat."

BAD closer: "We stand at an inflection point in how AI products are built — one that will reshape the future of work."

GOOD closer: "Suzy shipped in six weeks because the consolidation layer was already there. Most teams are still building it."

BAD closer: "The path forward requires bold thinking, decisive action, and a willingness to embrace what comes next."

GOOD closer: "Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem. Look at it again with that lens."

## Anti-patterns
- Closing with a thesis-style summary ("So as we've seen, context matters in three ways..."). The reader just read the piece. They saw it.
- Tacking on a faux-profound closer because the writer didn't trust the natural ending. Trust the natural ending.

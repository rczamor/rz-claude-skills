# Never-Use Terminology

## What it is
The list of terms that must not appear in Riché's drafts. These terms are either empty (carry no specific meaning), overused (pattern-match to generic AI thought leadership), or manufactured (sound technical without naming a real operation). When found in a draft, each term gets replaced with either a preferred term (see `terminology-preferred.md`) or with specific evidence — a number, a decision, a built thing.

The canonical never-use list:

- **AI-powered** — empty modifier; everything is AI-powered now, so the phrase tells the reader nothing
- **leverage** without an object — corporate filler verb; replace with use, deploy, build with, run on
- **game-changing** — empty superlative; see Fatal Fifteen #4
- **navigate the complexities of** — generic metaphor; see Fatal Fifteen #13
- **in today's rapidly evolving AI landscape** — generic opener; see Fatal Fifteen #1
- **context intelligence** — not an established term; manufactured jargon

## Why it matters
The never-use list catches the most common failure modes in one pass. "AI-powered," "leverage," "navigate," and "in today's..." are statistically the highest-frequency tells of generic AI thought leadership content. Cutting them removes the most pattern-matchable surface area. "Context intelligence" specifically gets banned because Riché writes about context architecture and someone will assume "context intelligence" is one of his preferred terms — it isn't, and the confusion would muddy the framework.

## How to apply
- Search every draft for each term on the never-use list. Replace each occurrence.
- "AI-powered" replacement is almost always a more specific verb or framing. "AI-powered insights" becomes "insights generated by [specific system]" or "the Decision Engine surfaces three insights per decision."
- "Leverage" without an object replacement: use, deploy, run on, build with. "Leverage AI" becomes "use AI" or "deploy AI in the consolidation layer."
- "Game-changing" replacement: a specific number or comparison. "Game-changing growth" becomes "300% growth at $0 CAC."
- "Navigate the complexities of" replacement: a specific verb. "Navigate the complexities of AI adoption" becomes "ship AI products that work" or "build the consolidation layer most teams skip."
- "In today's..." replacement: cut the entire opener and lead with a specific moment.
- "Context intelligence" replacement: "context architecture," "context system," or "context generation," depending on what the writer actually means.

## Examples
BAD: "Our AI-powered platform helps teams leverage cutting-edge insights to navigate the complexities of enterprise AI adoption."

GOOD: "Our platform runs the five-step context generation process on enterprise data. Teams that ship faster are the ones that already built the consolidation layer."

BAD: "In today's rapidly evolving AI landscape, context intelligence is becoming a game-changing capability."

GOOD: "Most AI products fail at the consolidation step. The teams that ship working systems are the ones that built the consolidation layer first. Models commoditize; context quality doesn't."

BAD: "Suzy leverages AI-powered context intelligence to deliver game-changing decisions."

GOOD: "Suzy's Decision Engine surfaces three insights per decision instead of thirty. The consolidation layer ranks by decision relevance, not search relevance. 350+ enterprise customers, six-week launch."

BAD: "Helm Labs is helping enterprise founders navigate the complexities of go-to-market with AI-powered context intelligence."

GOOD: "Helm Labs is sitting on $3.25M in pre-launch pipeline at 14x ACV. The pipeline exists because the context system convinces buyers we already understand their problem."

## Anti-patterns
- Reaching for a slightly fancier never-use term ("AI-driven" instead of "AI-powered"). Same problem with new paint.
- Defending a never-use term because it's "the industry vocabulary." The industry vocabulary is the problem.

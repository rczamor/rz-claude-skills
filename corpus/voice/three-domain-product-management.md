# Product Management (30%)

## What it is
The Product Management domain is the second-largest share of Riché's content — 30% of the rolling mix. The frame is "This is what I do." Content in this domain covers PM craft: discovery, prioritization, roadmap strategy, lifecycle management, metrics and outcomes, monetization decisions, the difference between research and discovery, what shipping at speed actually requires. The substance comes from Riché's actual PM work at Suzy, Helm Labs, Grandstage, and IBM, plus the operating principles he teaches in office hours.

## Why it matters
Holding 30% of the content on PM practice keeps the voice grounded as a practitioner who ships, not a commentator who comments on shipping. The three-domain framework specifically pairs Context/AI with PM because most context-architecture decisions are PM decisions in disguise — when to invest in the consolidation layer, how to sequence the rebuild, what to deprioritize so the upstream work can happen. The PM share is also what makes the content credible to product audiences who don't read AI commentary as their primary diet.

## How to apply
- Roughly 30% of the rolling four-week content mix should center on PM craft, decisions, frameworks, or specific shipping stories.
- Lead with the decision, not the framework. Frameworks are useful, but PM content lands harder when it leads with a specific tradeoff Riché made — what he chose, what he gave up, what he learned.
- Connect to Context/AI when the substance allows. Suzy's Decision Engine is a PM story (six-week launch) and a Context/AI story (consolidation layer). The intersection pieces are the most valuable.
- Pull frameworks from the `product-management` skill when relevant: discovery vs. research, RICE prioritization, the lifecycle phases, the Forbes AI 50 competency map, Riché's six forcing questions.
- Thursday Frameworks are often PM-domain. The format aligns with PM practice — name the framework, explain the problem, walk through application, give one real example.

## Examples
GOOD PM piece: "Discovery is not the same as research. Discovery is the work that produces a product decision. Research is one input. Most teams I've worked with confuse the two and ship slower because of it. At Suzy, we cut discovery cycle time in half by separating the two — research feeds discovery; discovery doesn't wait on research."

GOOD PM Hot Take: "If your roadmap doesn't have a deprioritized list, it's not a roadmap. It's a wish list."

GOOD PM Wednesday Deep Dive: A 900-word walkthrough of how Suzy's Decision Engine got prioritized — the six-week timeline, the deprioritization decisions that created room, the consolidation layer that was already built, the launch operations, the 350+ enterprise customer rollout. Specific decisions, named tools, actual metrics.

GOOD PM Thursday Framework: The five forcing questions Riché asks before approving a roadmap addition, with one example of a decision each question reframed.

GOOD intersection piece (PM + Context/AI): "Helm Labs is sitting on $3.25M in pre-launch pipeline at 14x ACV. Nothing has shipped. The pipeline exists because the context system convinces buyers we already understand their problem. That's a PM bet — investing in context before product — and most PMs won't make it because the ROI shows up months after the work."

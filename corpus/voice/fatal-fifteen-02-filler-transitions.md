# Fatal Fifteen #2: Skip Filler Transitions

## What it is
The second Fatal Fifteen rule. Filler transitions — "Moreover," "Furthermore," "Additionally," "That being said," "It's worth noting" — must not appear in any draft. These words signal that the writer is bridging between paragraphs out of habit, not because the next idea genuinely depends on the previous one. They make the prose sound assembled by template instead of written by a person with something to say.

## Why it matters
Filler transitions flatten the rhythm of a piece and signal generic AI-thought-leader register. They're the verbal equivalent of stage directions left in the script. When a reader sees "Moreover," they know the next sentence is going to be a restatement or a pile-on, not a turn. Riché's voice depends on rhythm and earned transitions — a one-line paragraph after a long one, a hard pivot, a colon that does the connective work. Filler transitions undo that.

## How to apply
- Search every draft for "Moreover," "Furthermore," "Additionally," "That being said," "It's worth noting." Delete each occurrence.
- After deleting, read the surrounding paragraphs aloud. If the connection still works, ship it. If not, the paragraphs needed a real transition, not a filler one — write the actual logical connection.
- When two ideas genuinely accumulate, the better moves are: a colon, a full stop and a new short paragraph, or a sentence that names the connection ("That's the easy half. The hard half is consolidation.").
- Same rule for the variants: "On top of that," "What's more," "In addition to the above."

## Examples
BAD: "Context architecture matters for product outcomes. Moreover, it shapes unit economics."

GOOD: "Context architecture matters for product outcomes. It also shapes unit economics — at Grandstage, $0 CAC was the context system doing the work upstream."

BAD: "We hit 300% user growth at Grandstage. Furthermore, the CAC was zero. Additionally, retention held."

GOOD: "We hit 300% user growth at Grandstage. CAC was zero. Retention held. The context system did all three."

BAD: "Most teams treat data as context. That being said, it's worth noting that some teams have started to differentiate."

GOOD: "Most teams treat data as context. A few teams have started to differentiate. Those few are the ones with defensible products."

## Anti-patterns
- Replacing one filler transition with another ("Moreover" becomes "Additionally"). Same problem.
- Keeping "It's worth noting that..." because the noting is genuinely worth doing. If it's worth noting, just note it. Skip the meta-frame.

# Context Layers and AI (50%)

## What it is
The Context Layers and AI domain is the largest share of Riché's content — 50% of the rolling mix. The frame is "This is what I know." Content in this domain centers on context architecture, the five-step context generation process (curate, synthesize, consolidate, prioritize, store intelligently), the failure modes of treating data as context, and the implications for AI products that hit production. The Hook ("Data is not context") and the supporting one-liners ("Retrieval without consolidation is just efficient delivery of noise") all live in this domain.

## Why it matters
This is the domain where Riché is most differentiated from the AI thought leadership cohort. Most commentators talk about models, prompts, agents, and tooling. Few have built and shipped context systems at scale and measured the outcomes — Grandstage at 300% growth, Suzy's Decision Engine at 350+ enterprise customers, Helm Labs at $3.25M pre-launch pipeline, the Context Layer Engine in development. Holding this domain at 50% of the content mix is what establishes Riché's position as a context architecture authority rather than a general AI commentator.

## How to apply
- Half of the rolling four-week content mix should center on context architecture, the five-step process, or the failure modes of teams that skip consolidation.
- Lead with built systems. Every Context/AI piece should reference at least one specific implementation: Grandstage, Helm Labs, Suzy, IBM, Context Layer Engine, or Microsoft Access for Boston Beer Company in 2003.
- Anchor with the Hook. "Data is not context" is the canonical provocation; the one-liners ("Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem.") provide rotation.
- One cognitive science insight per Context/AI piece is the maximum. Selectively cite — don't pile on. The science grounds the claim; the built system is the proof.
- Tuesday Signals are usually Context/AI domain. The lens for reacting to AI news is the context generation frame.

## Examples
GOOD Context/AI piece: "Most AI products fail at the consolidation step. Retrieval works. Synthesis works. Then the system dumps everything on the user and calls it context. We saw this at Grandstage before the rebuild — the dashboard was technically correct and operationally useless. Once we added consolidation, 300% user growth followed at $0 CAC. The system shipped less to the user, not more."

GOOD Context/AI Hot Take: "Models commoditize. Context quality doesn't. That's the moat."

GOOD Context/AI Tuesday Signal: "GPT-5 dropped this week. Faster, cheaper, smarter. The teams that benefit are the ones who already fixed their context layer. Everyone else is upgrading the engine on a car with the wrong fuel."

GOOD Context/AI Wednesday Deep Dive: A 900-word walkthrough of the five-step context generation process with the specific architectural decisions from Grandstage and Suzy, three named tools, two metrics, and one cognitive science insight (Hick's Law on choice paralysis applied to AI UX).

GOOD Context/AI Friday Story: The story of the rebuild that produced Grandstage's 300% growth — the moment the team realized retrieval wasn't enough, the architectural fight, the consolidation layer that fell out of it. Distilled lesson: "Data is not context. We had to lose six months to learn it."

# Fatal Fifteen #4: No Empty Superlatives

## What it is
The fourth Fatal Fifteen rule. Empty superlatives — "game-changing," "revolutionary," "unprecedented," and the rest of the cohort — must not appear in any draft. These words tell the reader the writer thinks something is important without giving the reader any way to verify it. They are credibility-destroyers because they signal that the writer is asking for the reader's reaction instead of supplying the evidence that would earn it.

## Why it matters
Every reader of AI content has been trained to discount these words to zero. "Revolutionary" appears in literally every product launch. "Game-changing" is the laziest possible compliment. "Unprecedented" gets attached to anything new, which means it's attached to everything. When Riché uses one of these words, the reader's instinct is to tune out the rest of the paragraph. The voice is calibrated to do the opposite — replace the empty superlative with a specific number, a specific decision, or a specific outcome.

## How to apply
- Search every draft for "game-changing," "revolutionary," "unprecedented," "transformational," "world-class," "industry-leading," "best-in-class." Delete and replace with specific evidence.
- The replacement is almost always a number, a name, or a comparison. "Game-changing" becomes "300% user growth." "Revolutionary" becomes "the consolidation step nobody else does." "Unprecedented" becomes "we shipped in six weeks; the previous attempt took eighteen months."
- If you can't find specific evidence to replace the superlative, the claim shouldn't be in the piece. The superlative was a placeholder for substance the writer didn't have.

## Examples
BAD: "Grandstage achieved game-changing user growth."

GOOD: "Grandstage hit 300% user growth at $0 CAC."

BAD: "Suzy launched a revolutionary Decision Engine."

GOOD: "Suzy shipped the Decision Engine in six weeks to 350+ enterprise customers — Intelligence, Insights, Impact pillars on one consolidation layer."

BAD: "Helm Labs has unprecedented pre-launch traction."

GOOD: "Helm Labs is sitting on a $3.25M pre-launch pipeline at 14x ACV. Nothing has shipped."

BAD: "The Context Layer Engine is a transformational personal knowledge system."

GOOD: "The Context Layer Engine is the architecture I'm using on myself: curate, synthesize, consolidate, prioritize, store intelligently. Same five steps Grandstage runs on its market data."

## Anti-patterns
- Trading "game-changing" for a slightly fancier superlative ("paradigm-shifting"). Same problem, longer word.
- Defending the superlative because the thing really is impressive. If it's impressive, the specific number or decision will say so.

# "Data Is Not Context" — Anchor Provocation

## What it is
"Data is not context" is the canonical Hook for Riché's Context/AI domain. It is a provocation, not a thesis — a sharp four-word claim that names the central failure mode most AI products fall into. The thesis is the work that follows: the five-step context generation process (curate, synthesize, consolidate, prioritize, store intelligently) and what it takes to actually transform raw data into decision-ready context. The Hook starts the conversation; the substance does the convincing.

## Why it matters
Most AI products fail at the same step, and most teams don't have language for it. "Data is not context" gives them the language. The phrase is short enough to remember, sharp enough to disagree with, and specific enough to lead directly into the work. Once a reader has the phrase, they have the lens — they can apply it to their own product and see the failure mode for themselves. That's what makes it a Hook rather than just a tagline. It's the entry point into the framework, and the framework does the rest.

## How to apply
- Use "Data is not context" as the provocation that opens or anchors a piece. It works as the first line of a Hot Take, as a section header in a Deep Dive, or as a one-line paragraph break inside a longer piece.
- Don't repeat it within the same piece more than twice. The Hook earns its weight from rarity within a single read.
- Always pair the Hook with the substance — the five-step process, a built-system proof point, or a specific failure mode. The Hook alone is provocation; the Hook plus substance is argument.
- Don't paraphrase the Hook. The exact wording — "Data is not context" — is what the audience accumulates familiarity with. Variants ("Data isn't the same as context") dilute the canonical phrase.
- The Hook is the anchor; the supporting one-liners (`hook-retrieval-without-consolidation.md`, `hook-context-problem-not-model.md`, etc.) are the rotation. Use them in different pieces so the Hook stays fresh.

## Examples
GOOD (Hot Take with Hook as opener): "Data is not context.

Most AI products fail at this step. They retrieve more data, hoping more becomes meaning. It doesn't. The fix is consolidation — taking what was retrieved and reducing it to the decision-ready slice the user needs. We learned this at Grandstage when 300% growth at $0 CAC came from the upstream work."

GOOD (Wednesday Deep Dive with Hook as section anchor):
"## Data is not context

The first failure mode is the assumption that more data produces more meaning. It doesn't. The mechanism is information theory: signal-to-noise ratio degrades as the data set grows unless the noise is filtered out. AI products that pile retrieval on retrieval are optimizing for recall and paying for it in usefulness. The fix isn't better retrieval; it's the consolidation step most teams skip..."

GOOD (Friday Story with Hook as distilled lesson): "...we lost six months on the V1 build before the pattern was obvious. Data is not context. We had to learn it the slow way."

BAD (paraphrase): "Data and context are not the same thing."

BAD (overused within one piece): "Data is not context. Most AI products fail because data is not context. The lesson is that data is not context. Data is not context."

## Anti-patterns
- Using the Hook without any substance underneath. The Hook is the provocation; without the framework or proof point, it reads as a slogan.
- Replacing the canonical wording with a "fresher" variant. The audience accumulates fluency; the variants undermine the accumulation.

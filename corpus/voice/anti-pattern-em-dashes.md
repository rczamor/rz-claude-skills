# No Em Dashes

## What it is
Em dashes — long dashes that set off parenthetical phrases or pivot mid-sentence — are not used in Riché's voice. Use colons or commas instead. The em dash has become a near-universal tell of LLM-generated prose; even when used by a human, it pattern-matches to the AI register that the rest of the voice rules are designed to avoid.

## Why it matters
The em dash is a powerful punctuation mark when used sparingly by a writer who has earned it. In LLM-era prose, it's been overused into uselessness — every AI-generated paragraph has two of them. When a reader sees an em dash now, the first instinct is to suspect machine generation. Riché's voice can't afford that suspicion. The replacement options (colon, comma, full stop into a new sentence) preserve the rhythm work the em dash was doing without triggering the pattern match.

## How to apply
- Search every draft for em dashes (—) and en dashes (–) used as em dashes. Replace each one with: a colon (when introducing a list or expansion), a comma (when setting off a parenthetical phrase), or a full stop (when the second clause is strong enough to stand alone).
- The full stop replacement is often the best choice; it forces the writer to commit to two real sentences instead of one bloated one.
- Hyphens (-) used to join compound modifiers ("decision-ready," "five-step") are fine. The rule applies only to em-dash usage.
- The rule applies to all formats — LinkedIn posts, articles, X posts, speaker bios. No exceptions.

## Examples
BAD: "Most teams treat data as context — they retrieve more, hoping more becomes meaning — but it doesn't."

GOOD (full stop): "Most teams treat data as context. They retrieve more, hoping more becomes meaning. It doesn't."

GOOD (comma): "Most teams treat data as context, retrieving more in the hope that more becomes meaning. It doesn't."

BAD: "Suzy shipped the Decision Engine in six weeks — 350+ enterprise customers — because the consolidation layer was already built."

GOOD (colon): "Suzy shipped the Decision Engine in six weeks to 350+ enterprise customers. The consolidation layer was already built."

BAD: "The five-step process — curate, synthesize, consolidate, prioritize, store intelligently — does the work retrieval can't."

GOOD (colon): "The five-step process does the work retrieval can't: curate, synthesize, consolidate, prioritize, store intelligently."

BAD: "Grandstage hit 300% user growth at $0 CAC — the model wasn't the differentiator — the context system was."

GOOD: "Grandstage hit 300% user growth at $0 CAC. The model wasn't the differentiator. The context system was."

## Anti-patterns
- Replacing the em dash with parentheses. Parentheses solve the LLM-tell problem but introduce a different parenthetical-overuse problem. Prefer the full stop.
- Defending the em dash because it's "rhythmically right." The rhythm can be preserved with a colon or full stop; the LLM-tell can't be undone.

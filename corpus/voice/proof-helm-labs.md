# Helm Labs Proof Point

## What it is
Helm Labs is the pre-launch PM and context-system proof point. The headline numbers: **$3.25M pre-launch pipeline, 14x ACV (pre-launch pipeline only, nothing shipped)**. The work demonstrates that a context system, built before the product, can convince enterprise buyers that the product team understands their problem deeply enough to be worth a commitment ahead of GA. Helm Labs is the proof that context-as-architecture pays off even before the first feature ships.

## Why it matters
The Helm Labs numbers stress-test a claim that's hard to make from Grandstage alone — that context architecture isn't just a post-launch optimization but a pre-launch differentiator. $3.25M in pipeline against zero shipped product is unusual; 14x ACV against a pre-launch baseline is very unusual. Both numbers require the buyer to be convinced of something other than feature parity. The thing they're convinced of is the context system — the work the team has done to understand the buyer's problem deeply. This proof point is also the cleanest example of practitioner-first credibility for Riché's PM domain.

## How to weave it in
- The honesty constraint is built into the proof point: "pre-launch pipeline only, nothing shipped." Always include this qualifier. Skipping it would imply revenue or shipped product, which isn't true.
- Use Helm Labs when the claim is about pre-launch execution, context-system-as-differentiator, or PM bets that pay off before the product does.
- The headline pair travels together: $3.25M and 14x ACV. The dollar figure shows scale; the multiple shows quality.
- For PM-domain content, Helm Labs is the canonical example of investing in context architecture before the product is shippable. The bet is unusual; the payoff is the proof.
- Pair with Grandstage when making the point that the context-system thesis works at multiple stages of the product lifecycle.

## Examples
GOOD short use: "Helm Labs is sitting on $3.25M in pre-launch pipeline at 14x ACV. Nothing has shipped. The pipeline exists because the context system convinces buyers we already understand their problem."

GOOD medium use (in a PM Hot Take): "Most PMs won't make a context-first bet because the ROI shows up months after the work. Helm Labs is sitting on $3.25M in pre-launch pipeline at 14x ACV — pipeline only, nothing shipped — because we made that bet. The context system did the convincing the product wasn't there to do yet."

GOOD long use (in a Thursday Framework on pre-launch PM execution): A 200-word section walking through the Helm Labs sequence — the decision to build the context system before the product, the discovery rounds that fed it, the buyer conversations that turned on the system's specificity, and the $3.25M pipeline outcome at 14x ACV.

GOOD intersection use (PM + Context/AI): "Two proof points for the context-as-moat thesis. Grandstage: 300% user growth at $0 CAC, post-launch. Helm Labs: $3.25M in pre-launch pipeline at 14x ACV, nothing shipped. Same architecture, different lifecycle stage. Both show the same thing — context is the work that pays earlier and longer than feature parity."

## Anti-patterns
- Citing Helm Labs as if the product has shipped. It hasn't. The qualifier "nothing shipped" is non-negotiable.
- Using Helm Labs for non-PM, non-Context/AI claims. The proof point's specificity is the asset; generic uses dilute it.

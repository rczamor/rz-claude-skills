# Grandstage Proof Point

## What it is
Grandstage is the canonical Context/AI proof point in Riché's portfolio. The headline numbers: **300% user growth, $0 CAC, context system for market intelligence**. The work is the working demonstration of the five-step context generation process applied to market data — curate, synthesize, consolidate, prioritize, store intelligently — producing decision-ready context for ICP segments. Grandstage is what Riché points to when claiming "context is the moat."

## Why it matters
Most AI thought leaders have to argue the context-architecture thesis on first principles. Grandstage lets Riché skip the argument and point at numbers. 300% user growth at $0 CAC is a pair of metrics that doesn't happen accidentally; it requires the upstream work to do real lift. When the audience pushes back on "is consolidation really the moat?", Grandstage is the receipt. The proof point is also load-bearing for the broader voice — it establishes that Riché ships, measures, and produces outcomes, not just commentary.

## How to weave it in
- Use Grandstage when the claim is about context architecture producing measurable outcomes — growth, CAC, retention, unit economics.
- Lead with the numbers when possible. "Grandstage hit 300% user growth at $0 CAC because..." is a stronger opening than "At Grandstage, we built a context system."
- The headline numbers travel together. Don't cite "300% growth" without the "$0 CAC" — the pair is what makes the metric defensible.
- For Wednesday Deep Dives, walk through the architectural decisions: which curation rules were used, how synthesis worked across data sources, what the consolidation layer reduced to per ICP.
- For Hot Takes and Tuesday Signals, the metric is the closer or the punch — "Grandstage hit 300% at zero CAC. The system shipped less, not more."

## Examples
GOOD short use: "Grandstage hit 300% user growth at $0 CAC. The model wasn't the differentiator. The context system was."

GOOD medium use (in a Tuesday Signal): "Most teams will read this and try to optimize retrieval. Wrong layer. We hit 300% growth at Grandstage by adding consolidation downstream of retrieval — the slice that gets shipped to the user. Same data; better context."

GOOD long use (in a Wednesday Deep Dive): A 200-word section walking through the Grandstage rebuild — the moment the team realized retrieval wasn't enough, the architectural fight, the consolidation layer that fell out of it, the per-ICP ranking logic, and the 300% / $0 CAC outcome that followed.

GOOD intersection use (Context/AI + Leadership): "Twenty years of building data systems and the lesson keeps showing up: the consolidation step is the one nobody wants to do. At Boston Beer in 2003, at IBM in the 2010s, at Grandstage in 2024 when 300% growth at $0 CAC came from the upstream work. Same pattern, different tech stack."

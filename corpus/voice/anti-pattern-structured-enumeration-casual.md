# No Structured Enumeration in Casual Posts

## What it is
Numbered lists, bullet points, and structured-enumeration markers ("First... Second... Third...") are not used in casual posts — Monday Hot Takes, Tuesday Signals, most Friday Stories, and X posts. Structured enumeration belongs in Thursday Frameworks and Wednesday Deep Dives, where the format genuinely earns its keep. In casual posts, the structure pattern-matches to corporate listicle and undercuts the conversational register.

## Why it matters
Casual posts work because they read like one person thinking out loud. A numbered list breaks that voice — it imposes a structure that the prose didn't earn. Worse, structured enumeration is one of the most common AI-thought-leader patterns: "Here are 5 reasons why..." / "3 lessons I learned about...". The reader pattern-matches the format and tunes out before the content. Riché's casual posts use prose rhythm — short paragraphs, hard pivots, single-line punches — to do the work that bullets pretend to do.

## How to apply
- Audit every casual draft for numbered lists or bullets. If found, convert to prose with paragraph breaks doing the structural work.
- The exception: Thursday Frameworks legitimately need numbered structure when walking through a multi-step process. A 5-step framework can use numbered steps. A 200-word Hot Take cannot.
- Even within a Framework, the prose between the numbered steps should not be bulleted. The numbered headings carry the structure; the explanation underneath should be conversational.
- "First, second, third" used as inline markers in flowing prose is borderline. Use sparingly; prefer paragraph breaks.

## Examples
BAD (Hot Take with bullets):
"Most AI products fail at context. Here's why:
- They treat data as context
- They skip the consolidation step
- They optimize retrieval instead of meaning
- They ship more to the user, not less"

GOOD (Hot Take in prose):
"Most AI products fail at context. They treat data as context, skip the consolidation step, optimize retrieval instead of meaning, and ship more to the user when they should ship less. Each one of those is the same mistake at a different layer."

BAD (Tuesday Signal with numbered list):
"3 takeaways from this week's GPT-5 announcement:
1. Faster
2. Cheaper
3. Smarter
But what does it mean for builders?"

GOOD (Tuesday Signal in prose):
"GPT-5 dropped this week. Faster, cheaper, smarter. The teams that benefit are the ones who already fixed their context layer. Everyone else is upgrading the engine on a car with the wrong fuel."

GOOD (Thursday Framework with structure):
"The five-step context generation process:

**1. Curate.** Filter the inputs at ingest. Most teams skip this and pay for it downstream.
**2. Synthesize.** Combine across sources before the model sees the data...
[rest of framework]"

## Anti-patterns
- Converting prose to bullets because "the post is too long to read." Long prose with rhythm reads faster than bullets that fragment the argument.
- Using bullets for "scannability" in a 200-word post. The post is already scannable; bullets add visual noise without adding readability.

# Fatal Fifteen #15: No "Key Takeaway" Summary Blocks

## What it is
The fifteenth and final Fatal Fifteen rule. "Key Takeaway:", "Bottom Line:", "TL;DR:", and "Main Point:" summary blocks must not appear in any draft. These blocks are the most aggressive signal of generic thought-leadership content — they assume the reader can't extract meaning from the prose and need a distilled bullet at the end. Riché's voice trusts the reader to read.

## Why it matters
A "Key Takeaway" block tells the reader two things, both bad: (1) the prose above it didn't communicate the takeaway clearly enough on its own, and (2) the writer thinks the reader needed a study guide. Both undercut the piece. The takeaway should be embedded in the strongest sentence in the piece, or arrive as the natural closing line — not labeled and offset like a homework summary. When a piece genuinely needs a one-line distillation, write it as the closer, without the label.

## How to apply
- Search every draft for "Key Takeaway," "Bottom Line," "TL;DR," "Main Point," "Summary," "In conclusion." Cut the label and the offset block. Keep the substance only if it's genuinely the strongest sentence in the piece — and then move it inline.
- The natural closer of a Riché piece is usually a one-line distillation of the lesson. That's the takeaway. It doesn't need a label.
- Wednesday Deep Dives, Thursday Frameworks, and Friday Stories all close without labeled summary blocks. The structure of the piece does the summarizing.
- The exception is rare: a Thursday Framework that reuses the same structure across multiple weeks may benefit from a labeled callout — but even then, "Step 4:" is structural, not summary.

## Examples
BAD closing block:
"**Key Takeaway:** Context architecture is the moat. Models commoditize, but consolidation work compounds. Teams that invest in the upstream layer win."

GOOD closing line: "Models commoditize. Context quality doesn't. That's the moat."

BAD closing block:
"**Bottom Line:** Suzy launched the Decision Engine in six weeks because the consolidation layer was already built. Most teams won't ship that fast because they haven't done the upstream work."

GOOD closing line: "Suzy shipped in six weeks because the consolidation layer was already there. Most teams are still building it."

BAD closing block:
"**TL;DR:** Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem. Fix the context first, then optimize the model."

GOOD closing line: "Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem. Look at it again with that lens."

## Anti-patterns
- Labeling the closer "Key Insight" instead of "Key Takeaway." Same problem with a different label.
- Including a TL;DR at the top of a short post. If the post is short enough that a TL;DR fits, the post is already its own TL;DR.

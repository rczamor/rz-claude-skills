# Practitioner-First

## What it is
Practitioner-first is the second of Riché's five core voice attributes: every claim is grounded in something he built, shipped, or measured. He doesn't comment on AI from the outside; he writes from inside the work — from the actual architectural decisions, the actual user growth numbers, the actual six-week platform transformations. The voice belongs to someone whose hands are on the system, not someone narrating what other people are doing.

## Why it matters
The AI conversation in 2025-2026 is dominated by commentators — people summarizing trends, restating frameworks they read elsewhere, posting takes on news without ever having shipped a system that touches the problem. Practitioner-first is the differentiator that makes Riché's content credible to other practitioners. If a post could have been written by someone who only reads about AI, it doesn't pass. The proof is in the proof points: Grandstage, Helm Labs, IBM, Suzy, Context Layer Engine, Microsoft Access for Boston Beer Company in 2003.

## How to apply
- Every public-facing piece should reference at least one built thing — a system, a metric, a decision, a named tool.
- If a claim is abstract, follow it within two paragraphs with a concrete instance from Riché's portfolio.
- When tempted to cite an industry trend, ask: have I done this myself? If yes, lead with that. If no, either skip the claim or attribute the source clearly.
- Match the proof point to the claim's domain — context architecture claims pull from Grandstage and Context Layer Engine; PM claims pull from Suzy and IBM; longevity claims pull from the Boston Beer Company work in 2003.

## Examples
GOOD: "I'll tell you what consolidation looks like in production. At Suzy, we shipped the Decision Engine in six weeks because we'd already built the consolidation layer. 350+ enterprise customers got Intelligence, Insights, and Impact pillars working together — not because the model was special, but because the context was."

BAD: "Industry leaders agree that consolidation is the missing layer in modern AI systems."

GOOD: "Helm Labs is sitting on a $3.25M pre-launch pipeline at 14x ACV. Nothing has shipped yet. The pipeline exists because the context system convinces buyers we already understand their problem."

BAD: "Pre-launch pipelines are increasingly common in B2B AI."

## Anti-patterns
- Citing other people's work as if it's evidence for Riché's claim. Other practitioners' wins support a trend; only Riché's own work supports his thesis.
- Generic "I've seen teams do this" framing. Replace with the specific team and outcome.

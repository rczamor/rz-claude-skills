# Suzy Proof Point

## What it is
Suzy is the canonical PM-and-Context/AI intersection proof point. The headline numbers: **Decision Engine launch (Intelligence/Insights/Impact pillars), 350+ enterprise customers, platform transformation in six weeks**. The work demonstrates that when the consolidation layer is built upstream, a major product launch can ship in six weeks instead of eighteen months — and that the speed itself is the moat.

## Why it matters
Suzy is the strongest evidence that context architecture isn't just a UX or growth story; it's a PM speed-to-market story. The six-week launch number is unusual for enterprise SaaS at the 350+ enterprise customer scale. Most teams would have shipped this in twelve to eighteen months. Suzy didn't because the consolidation layer — the unglamorous middle piece between retrieval and the user — was already built. The launch was just operationalizing what was there. This proof point is what makes Riché's "context is the moat" thesis credible to PM and engineering audiences who need to see speed-to-market evidence.

## How to weave it in
- Use Suzy when the claim is about PM execution speed, enterprise launch velocity, or the way upstream architectural bets pay off in shipping cadence.
- The pillar names — Intelligence, Insights, Impact — anchor the launch in a concrete product structure. Use them when the substance benefits from the specifics.
- The "six weeks" number is the emphasis point. It's the part that surprises people. Lead with it when possible.
- The "350+ enterprise customers" anchors the scale. Pair with the six-week number; one carries speed, the other carries scale.
- For Wednesday Deep Dives, walk through the prior architectural work that made the six-week launch possible — the consolidation layer, the synthesis pipeline, the decisions that got deprioritized to make room.

## Examples
GOOD short use: "Suzy shipped the Decision Engine in six weeks because the consolidation layer was already built. 350+ enterprise customers got Intelligence, Insights, and Impact on day one. Most teams won't ship that fast because they haven't done the upstream work."

GOOD medium use (in a PM Hot Take): "If your last major launch took eighteen months, the bottleneck wasn't engineering. It was the architecture you skipped two years earlier. Suzy shipped Decision Engine in six weeks to 350+ enterprise customers because the consolidation layer was there before the launch was scoped."

GOOD long use (in a Thursday Framework on PM speed-to-market): A 200-word section walking through the Suzy six-week launch — the upstream work that preceded it, the deprioritization decisions, the launch sequence, the rollout to 350+ enterprise customers, and the principle that emerged.

GOOD intersection use (Context/AI + PM): "Two stories show the same architecture pattern. Grandstage: 300% growth at $0 CAC, post-launch. Suzy: six-week launch to 350+ enterprise customers. Different products, different stages, same pattern — when the consolidation layer is upstream and ready, the visible work runs five to ten times faster."

## Anti-patterns
- Citing the launch without the six-week timeframe. The timeframe is the load-bearing fact; without it, the proof point loses its bite.
- Using Suzy for non-PM, non-Context/AI claims. The intersection is the asset; generic uses dilute it.

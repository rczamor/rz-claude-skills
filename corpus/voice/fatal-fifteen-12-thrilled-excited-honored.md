# Fatal Fifteen #12: No "I'm Thrilled/Excited/Honored to Announce..."

## What it is
The twelfth Fatal Fifteen rule. Announcement openers — "I'm thrilled to announce," "I'm excited to share," "I'm honored to be," "Beyond stoked to..." — must not open any draft. Even when the news is genuinely good, these openers signal generic LinkedIn announcement template and immediately flatten the piece. They are the verbal equivalent of confetti emoji at the top of a corporate press release.

## Why it matters
The reader has seen these openers thousands of times. They don't communicate excitement; they communicate that the writer reached for the default. Riché's voice handles announcements differently — the news arrives as the natural conclusion of a story, or as a sharp single sentence that lets the substance carry the weight. Announcements without these openers feel earned. Announcements with these openers feel like they're checking a box.

## How to apply
- Search every draft for "I'm thrilled," "I'm excited," "I'm honored," "Beyond stoked," "So pumped." Cut.
- The replacement is usually a story-driven opener — what happened, what was the decision, what shipped. The announcement becomes the second beat, after the context.
- For genuinely big announcements, a single declarative sentence carries more weight than any thrill claim. "We shipped it." "I'm joining X." "Helm Labs has $3.25M in pre-launch pipeline."
- The rule applies even to celebratory announcements — promotions, awards, speaking gigs. The substance carries it; the thrill signal undercuts it.

## Examples
BAD: "I'm thrilled to announce that Suzy has launched the Decision Engine to 350+ enterprise customers!"

GOOD: "Suzy shipped the Decision Engine this week. 350+ enterprise customers. Six weeks of platform work. The consolidation layer was already there; the launch was just operationalizing it."

BAD: "I'm excited to share that I'll be speaking at [Conference] on context architecture!"

GOOD: "I'm speaking at [Conference] in October on context architecture. The talk is the one I've wanted to give for a year — the failure modes I've watched four AI teams hit, in the same order, on the same step."

BAD: "Honored to have been recognized as one of the top voices in product management this year!"

GOOD: "Recognized this year as a top voice in product management. The recognition that means something is the practitioners who told me a piece changed how they thought about a problem."

BAD: "Beyond stoked to share that Helm Labs has hit $3.25M in pre-launch pipeline!"

GOOD: "Helm Labs is sitting on a $3.25M pre-launch pipeline at 14x ACV. Nothing has shipped. The pipeline exists because the context system convinces buyers we already understand their problem."

## Anti-patterns
- Substituting a slightly different announcement opener ("Pleased to share..." / "Delighted to announce..."). Same template.
- Defending the opener because "people expect this format." The default is the problem; deviating is the differentiator.

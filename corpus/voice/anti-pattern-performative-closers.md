# No Performative Closers

## What it is
Performative closers — "Been saying it.", "Just my two cents.", "Mic drop.", "There. I said it.", "Make it make sense." — are not used in Riché's voice. These one-liners are LinkedIn-influencer cohort tics that perform contrarianism without supplying substance. The sentence is the performance; there's nothing under it. Riché's voice uses real closers: a one-line distillation of the lesson, a reframed claim, or simply the last beat of the story.

## Why it matters
Performative closers are the single clearest signal of contrarianism-without-substance. They imply that the writer has been telling a hard truth that nobody wanted to hear, when in fact the writer just stated a generic claim and slapped a smug closer on it. Readers have been pattern-trained to discount these closers; they actively reduce the credibility of whatever came before. The substitute is a closer that does real work — naming the takeaway sharply enough that it doesn't need a victory lap.

## How to apply
- Search every draft's last paragraph for "Been saying it," "Just my two cents," "Mic drop," "Make it make sense," "I'll wait," "There. I said it." Cut the closer entirely; usually no replacement is needed.
- The closer that remains after cutting the performative line is usually the real closer. It's stronger without the smug tag.
- If the piece feels weak after the performative closer is cut, the substance was leaning on the closer for force. Strengthen the substance.
- The test: read the closer alone. If it could be applied to any post on any topic by any LinkedIn personality, it's performative. Cut.

## Examples
BAD: "Most AI products are doing context wrong. Been saying it."

GOOD: "Most AI products are doing context wrong. The fix is consolidation, not better retrieval. We learned this at Grandstage."

BAD: "Suzy shipped the Decision Engine in six weeks because the consolidation layer was built. Most teams won't do the upstream work. Make it make sense."

GOOD: "Suzy shipped the Decision Engine in six weeks because the consolidation layer was built. Most teams won't do the upstream work. They want the launch without the architecture."

BAD: "Models commoditize. Context quality doesn't. Just my two cents."

GOOD: "Models commoditize. Context quality doesn't. That's the moat."

BAD: "Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem. Mic drop."

GOOD: "Your AI doesn't have a model problem. It has a context problem. Look at it again with that lens."

## Anti-patterns
- Replacing "Been saying it" with a slightly less obvious smug tag ("Don't @ me"). Same problem in different costume.
- Defending the performative closer because the audience "expects punchy endings." Punchy endings come from substance, not labels.

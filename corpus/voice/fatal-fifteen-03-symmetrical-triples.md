# Fatal Fifteen #3: No Symmetrical Triples

## What it is
The third Fatal Fifteen rule. Symmetrical triples — three adjectives or phrases stacked in parallel rhythm, like "innovative, transformative, and groundbreaking" or "scalable, defensible, and durable" — must not appear in any draft. These constructions are the signature rhythm of generic thought-leadership prose. They sound like they're saying something. They aren't.

## Why it matters
Symmetrical triples create the illusion of substance through cadence. The reader's ear gets carried along by the parallel structure and doesn't notice that the three words are doing the same vague work. Worse, the triples almost always involve empty superlatives ("revolutionary," "game-changing," "unprecedented") or vague approvals ("powerful," "robust," "enterprise-grade") that wouldn't survive on their own. Riché's voice avoids the trick because the trick is the giveaway — every reader who has scrolled LinkedIn has been pattern-trained to skip past anything in this rhythm.

## How to apply
- Scan every draft for ", X, and Y" where X and Y are adjectives. If found, replace the triple with one specific word, or rewrite with a concrete claim.
- If the three words are doing different work, separate them into different sentences. Symmetry in service of contrast is fine. Symmetry as decoration is not.
- The test: if you remove two of the three adjectives, does the sentence lose meaning? If no, you had a triple. If yes, the structure was earning its keep.

## Examples
BAD: "We're building an innovative, transformative, and groundbreaking platform for enterprise AI."

GOOD: "We're building the consolidation layer most enterprise AI platforms skip."

BAD: "Context generation is scalable, defensible, and durable as a moat."

GOOD: "Context generation is the moat. It scales because the upstream work is reusable; it's defensible because the consolidation logic encodes domain expertise; it lasts because models commoditize and context quality doesn't."

BAD: "Suzy's Decision Engine is powerful, robust, and enterprise-grade."

GOOD: "Suzy's Decision Engine ships to 350+ enterprise customers. The Intelligence/Insights/Impact pillars share one consolidation layer. That's why it shipped in six weeks."

## Anti-patterns
- Replacing the triple with a pair ("innovative and transformative"). Slightly less obvious; same problem.
- Keeping the triple because it sounds polished. Polish is not the goal. Specificity is.

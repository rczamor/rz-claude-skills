# Fatal Fifteen #1: Never Open With "In Today's..."

## What it is
The first rule of the Fatal Fifteen. Any draft that opens with "In today's...", "In today's rapidly evolving AI landscape...", "In today's competitive market...", or any variant of that construction must be rewritten before it ships. This is the most common AI-thought-leader opener and the strongest signal that a piece could have been generated by anyone — which is the exact failure mode Riché's voice is calibrated against.

## Why it matters
The "In today's..." opener does three things at once, all bad: it stalls before the actual claim, it pattern-matches to every generic LinkedIn post the reader has scrolled past, and it sets a register that's impossible to recover from. By the time the second sentence arrives, the reader has already classified the piece as filler. Story-driven openers are the antidote. The lead has to be a specific moment, decision, or metric — not a general observation about the era we live in.

## How to apply
- Search every draft for "In today" before publishing. If found, delete the entire opening sentence and rewrite from the first specific moment in the piece.
- If the draft has no specific moment to lead with, the draft has a deeper problem — the substance is too abstract to publish as is.
- The replacement opener should pass the test: would this sentence make sense if it were the only sentence the reader saw? "In today's AI landscape..." doesn't. "Six weeks. That's how long it took to ship Suzy's Decision Engine." does.

## Examples
BAD: "In today's rapidly evolving AI landscape, context has become the critical differentiator."

GOOD: "Grandstage hit 300% user growth at $0 CAC. The model wasn't the differentiator. The context system was."

BAD: "In today's competitive enterprise software market, speed-to-deployment matters more than ever."

GOOD: "Six weeks. That's how long Suzy needed to ship the Decision Engine to 350+ enterprise customers."

BAD: "In today's world, where AI tools are everywhere, builders are asking better questions about context."

GOOD: "Last week I watched a founder tell me his AI product had a 'model problem.' It didn't. It had a context problem. They almost always do."

## Anti-patterns
- Replacing "In today's..." with "In our era..." or "In the current moment..." — same opener, different paint.
- Keeping the "In today's..." line because "the rest of the piece is good." The lead is load-bearing; if it's wrong, the piece is wrong.
